CHINMOYEE Exploring Simbiology and Matlab Discovered that Simbiology is an easy to use interface for modelling systems . Has a number of equations built in that can be used to model the system such as the Chemical kinetic equation , Hill kinetics , Iso-Uni-Uni and the Michealis-Menten equation etc. There are two ways to simulate : differential and stochastic There is more than one algorithm for the differential and stochastic simulations.- Implicit/Explict Tau , exact Stochastic , ode15 , ode45 etc . We will most likely be using Chemical kinetic equations to model our system. MANDY Teleportation in Second Life Tried out different scripts to make objects teleport users when touched. The first couple I tried did not teleport people to the right coordinates, but I soon determined that this was because I had entered them in the wrong order (oops). I made a teleportation grid that would correspond to different spheres. I also tried to make it so that people wouldn't have to sit in order to teleport, but I feel like that may be a requirement from what I've seen. I am still trying to figure out a way for the teleporter to remain inactive, or at least show that the location in question is being in use, because we don't want people interrupting others during their biobricking sessions. VICKI Modelling a repressilator We began to develop basic proficiencies with the SimBiology platform by working on an assignment that Anders prepared. It focused on the Repressilator - a 3-gene system for which transcription of one gene represses transcription of the subsequent gene, and so forth. With this, an oscillitory system of expression has been implemented in E. coli bacteria, as described by Elowitz et al. By implementing a model of this system in SimBiology, we gained a better understanding of the capabilities and computational power of this particular platform.
