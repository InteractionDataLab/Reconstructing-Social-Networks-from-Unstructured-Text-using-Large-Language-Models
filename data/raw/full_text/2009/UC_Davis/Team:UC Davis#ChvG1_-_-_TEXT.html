Studies have shown that ChvG /ChvI two component systems specifically get induced in an acidic environment (1).             As a result, there is growing consensus that the ChvG/ChvI, two-component regulatory system, may be the primary global pH sensor in Agrobacterium Tumefaciens and as such, serves in coordinating the expression of acid-induced genes (2).
