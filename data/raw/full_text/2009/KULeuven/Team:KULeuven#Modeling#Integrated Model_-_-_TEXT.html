Of course It's obvious that the system will not behave in a linear way. But the concepts and design strategies of linear control theory can be translated to non-linear control theory. One of the most useful ways of investigating the behaviour of closed loop system is the investigation of the open loop system. The open loop system is the system which one becomes if you 'remove' the differentiator. Block model of the system with proportional controller Stability One can show that the proportional gain can not be infinitely large due to stability problems, since we lose phase margin if we increase the proportional gain. Oscillations will become larger as can be seen on the graph below. Eventually the oscillations become dominant and will destabilize the controlled system. Stability of system, overshoot and oscillations increases with increasing gain of controller.
