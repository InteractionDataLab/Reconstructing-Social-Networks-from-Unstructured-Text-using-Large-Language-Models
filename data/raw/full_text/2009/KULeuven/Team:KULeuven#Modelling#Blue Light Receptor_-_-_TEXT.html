Under the influence of the intensity of a blue light, the RIBOKEY is transcribed. The light used to perform the test has a wavelength of 470nm. The biology works as follows: YcgF protein is believed to dimerize upon photo-excitation. The YcgE protein functions as a repressor for the transcription of the mRNA_RIBOKEY. However, the dimer form of YcgF is capable of interacting with the operator-bound YcgE, thereby releasing it from the DNA and starting transcription.
