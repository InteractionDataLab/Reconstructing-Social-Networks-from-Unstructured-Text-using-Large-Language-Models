Infusion Description

Infusion is a method to combine DNA fragments using a combination of PCR and homologous recombination. It can be used as an alternative to ligation with the advantage that it does not require restriction enzyme digests, although specific primers are required for the reaction.

Clonetech page on In-Fusion

Infusion Protocol

  1. PCR amplify the DNA sequence to be recombined into the vector using primers with sequences of the vector on 5' ends (refer to the Clonetech In-Fusion page above).
  2. Linearize the vector using appropriate restriction enzymes.
  3. Mix the purified PCR insert(50-200ng) and linearized vector(100-400ng).
  4. Add 1µl of 5x In-Fusion Reaction Buffer and MilliQ up tp 4.5µl.
  5. Add 0.5µl of In-Fusion Enzyme and mix well.
  6. Incubate at 37ºC for 15 min, followed by 15min ant 50ºC, then place on ice.
  7. Bring the reaction volume up to 25µl with TE buffer(pH8.0) and mix well.
  8. Use 5µl of reaction mixture for transformation.
