Software Taking images using the hardware and turning this into a holograph, then analysing this image to optimise co-cultures With the decreasing supply but increasing demand for fossil fuels, biofuels are a renewable alternative to help cope with our growing energy needs. We aim to develop and optimize a stable microbial co-culture system whereby the source of energy is light, and carbon flows from CO2 in the atmosphere to synthesize a biofuel. This simple synthetic microbial community will comprise Chlamydomonas, an algae, that will produce sugars through photosynthesis to feed the biofuel-producing E. coli, ideally resulting in a growth system that could reduce the cost of feedstock materials for biofuel production.
