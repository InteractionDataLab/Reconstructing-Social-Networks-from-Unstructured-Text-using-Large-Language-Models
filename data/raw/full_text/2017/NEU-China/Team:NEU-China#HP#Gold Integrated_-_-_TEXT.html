Consult Several Medical Experts About Detection of Early Cancer  Shugang Fan Medical PhD He is expert in a variety of benign tumors (liver and kidney hemangioma, uterine fibroids, breast fibroids) active treatment of traditional Chinese medicine, and the treatment by Chinese medicine after the radiotherapy and chemotherapy for some kinds of malignant tumors surgery, especially conditioning tumor pain, vomiting, diarrhea, ascites and other symptoms. Yan Yu Deputy Chief Physician, master in medical She is engaged in obstetrics and gynecology clinical more than 20 years, specializing in all kinds of perinatal medical diagnosis and treatment, uterine laparoscopy and other minimally invasive surgery, including the diagnosis and treatment of uterine fibroids, cervical cancer, pelvic disease and other cervical disease, published more than 10 papers. Finally, we contacted two experts of hospitals, told them about our project, and received some feedback. Doctor Fan, the attending physician in Fuzhou Guoyi Tang Chinese Medicine Hospital, said that at present, the diagnosis of the tumor through the puncture, biopsy production of pathological sections and other means of the patient itself has some damage, and may even cause the proliferation of tumor cells, aggravate the condition. And our project had application prospects, especially for the detection of diabetes. And Doctor Yu, the deputy Chief Physician in Chinese People 's Liberation Army 476th Hospital, said that it could be considered to ask the patient to blow to detect whether the exhaled VOC contained the associated disease odor, or to detect urine odor, but the interference was large. Inspiration to our project: • As the incipient disease emits very feeble VOCs, we designed the crispra-mediated olfactory signal amplification pathway and successfully enhanced the sensitivity of our biosensor to odor identification. • For some interferential smell caused by the fungi, because our olfactory receptor can only be combined with its specific odor, no matter what interferential odor exist, our ismeller can specifically identify the corresponding odor. • Because some tumor cells emit a variety of mixed odors, we intend to introduce multiple olfactory receptors into one cell to achieve specific detection of multiple odor to enhance the accuracy of cancer detection.
