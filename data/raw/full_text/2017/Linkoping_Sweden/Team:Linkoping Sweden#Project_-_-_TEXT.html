The cause of the disease is still largely unknown [1]. The polypeptide amyloid-beta and the protein tau are two molecules that are known to be related to the development of the disease. These are found in the brain in and around our neurons and in Alzheimer's disease, they accumulate to form plaques and tangles respectively [3, 4]. Because these proteins are prone to aggregate they are hard to study in a laboratory environment. The aim of the project is to optimize the expression of the amyloid-beta and tau in Escherichia coli. Chaperones are proteins that can help other proteins to fold into their native three-dimensional shape, if Amyloid-beta and Tau are correctly folded they are less likely to aggregate. We will study four different chaperones to determine the optimal combination of chaperone system for expressing Amyloid-beta and Tau respectively. With our project, we hope to make a new model for expressing these proteins without aggregation. We also see a future for our model to be used to express other difficult proteins. Sources 1.
