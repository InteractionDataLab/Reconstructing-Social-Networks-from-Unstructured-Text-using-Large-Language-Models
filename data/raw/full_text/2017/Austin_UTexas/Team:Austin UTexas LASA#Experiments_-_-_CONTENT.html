    PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction)

    Reactants

    10x Reaction Mix5 uL
    For primer (10 uM)1 uL
    Rev primer (10 uM)1 uL
    Template1 uL
    Taq Polymerase1 uL
    MgCl2 (25 mM)1 uL
    DMSO1 uL
    NF H2o41 uL
    Total50 uL
    Protocol
    1. On ice, add all reagents to PCR tube, Taq polymerase last.
    2. Run thermocycler program:
      • 95 C for 5 min (2 min if NOT using Hotstart)

    > 10 cycles: 95 C for 20 s 0.3 C/s to 50 C 72 C for # s (extension = 1 min/kb)

    > 15 cycles: 95 C for 20 s 55 C for 20 s 72 C for 1 min

    72 C for 10 min 4 C forever

    PCR Clean Up

    Reagents and protocol from Zymo DNA Clean & Concentrator Kit

    Reagents

    DNA Binding BufferRefer to table below.
    DNA Wash BufferRefer to table below.
    NF H2OVolume dependent on objective.

    Protocol:all centrifugation steps should be performed between 10,000 - 16,000g.

    1. On ice, add DNA Binding Buffer (2-7 volumes) and the sample (see table below) in a microcentrifuge tube and mix briefly by vortexing.
      2. ApplicationDNA Binding Buffer: SampleExample
      Genomic DNA (> 2 kb)2 : 1200 uL : 100 uL
      PCR Product, DNA Fragment5 : 1500 uL : 100 uL
    2. Transfer mixture to a minicolumn inside of a collection tube and centrifuge for 30 seconds, discard flow-through.
    3. Add 200 uL DNA Wash Buffer to the column and centrifuge for 30 seconds. Repeat 1x.
    4. Add water (volume dependent on objective) directly to the column, and incubate at room temperature for 1 min.
    5. Transfer the column to a clean microcentrifuge tube and centrifuge for 1 min to elute the DNA.

    Gels & Gel Electrophoresis

    Reagents:

    TAE Buffer~ 50 mL per gel, more necessary for electrophoresis chamber.
    NF Agarose (generally Seakem LE)Grams of agarose : mL buffer = percentage of gel
    Ethidium Bromide~ 1 drop

    **Don’t forget ladder. Protocol (gel):

    1. Mix the TAE buffer and agarose once measured in microwavable flask. Stir with stir bar in flask and magnetic stirrer.
    2. Afterwards, microwave for 1-3 mins until agarose is completely dissolved.
        Pause the microwave if the solution starts bubbling over, then stir the solution by slightly shaking the flask, and put the flask in the microwave again.
    3. Let the flask cool until it is cool enough to carry with the orange heat “gloves.”
    4. Add ethidium bromide and let gel solidify (10 - 20 min). Remember combs.
    5. Usually, use the gel for gel electrophoresis right away, but if needed, the gel can be stored in a container filled with TAE buffer and 1-2 drops of ethidium bromide.
    Protocol (gel electrophoresis):
    1. Add reagents together (always at least ladder and sample).
      • This can occur in PCR tubes or parafilm.
      # bp ladder - 2 uL Loading dye - 4 uL (1:3.5 ratio) Water - 8 uL Sample - volume depends on objective. Remember: try to have at least 50 ng to visualize on gel. Loading dye - 1:3 ratio with sample.
    2. Place the gel in the gel tray in the gel electrophoresis chamber and fill with TAE buffer to max line.
    3. Since DNA is negatively charged, the wells should be near the positive end (black) and DNA will “run to red.”
    4. Put the ladder and sample(s) in the wells with the ladder in Well 1, etc. Close the chamber properly, and run the gel electrophoresis chamber at 100 - 130 V.
    5. Wait for about 30 minutes before stopping the gel electrophoresis chamber and removing the gel tray.
    6. Place the gel in the Gel Imager (UV Light) next to the computer that will show the image. Run the program “Ethidium Bromide” to see DNA bands.
    7. Make sure to position the gel in the center.
    8. Remember: smaller fragments are farther away from the wells.
    9. Clean up area, making sure to throw the gel away in the gel waste bin.

    Gel Purification

    Reagents

    Membrane Binding Solution1 mL per each g of gel
    Membrane Wash Solution1200 uL
    NF water15 uL

    Protocol

    1. Weigh a microcentrifuge tube for each DNA fragment to be isolated and record weight
    2. Use a UV lamp to excise the DNA fragment of interest in a minimal volume of agarose using a clean scalpel or razor blade
    3. Transfer the gel slice to the weighed microcentrifuge tube and record the weight
    4. Subtract the weight of the empty tube from the total weight to obtain the weight of the gel slice
    5. The gel slice may be stored at 4°C or at –20°C for up to one week in a tightly closed tube under nuclease-free conditions before continuing purification
    6. Add Membrane Binding Solution to the gel slice
    7. Vortex the mixture and incubate at 50–65°C for 10 minutes or until the gel slice is completely dissolved
    8. Vortex the tube every few minutes to increase the rate of agarose gel melting
    9. Centrifuge the tube briefly at room temperature to ensure the contents are at the bottom of the tube
    10. Once the agarose gel is melted, the gel will not resolidify at room temperature
    11. Transfer the dissolved gel mixture or prepared PCR product to the SV Minicolumn assembly
    12. Incubate for 1 minute at room temperature before centrifugation for 1 minute and discarding supernatant
    13. Wash with 700µL of Membrane Wash Solution, centrifuge for 1 minute, and discard supernatant
    14. Wash with 500µL of Membrane Wash Solution, centrifuge for 5 minutes, and discard supernatant
    15. Centrifuge 1 minute with the microcentrifuge lid open to allow evaporation of any residual ethanol
    16. Transfer SV Minicolumn to a new microcentrifuge tube
    17. Add Nuclease Free water directly to the center of the column
    18. Membrane should be completely covered with nuclease-free water
    19. Incubate at room temperature for 1 minute, centrifuge for 1 minute at 16,000 × g, and repeat
    20. Discard the SV Minicolumn
    21. Store the tube containing the eluted DNA at 4°C or –20°C
    22. Check the purity of DNA in the nanodrop
    23. If concentration not high enough, speed vac and nanodrop again

    Transformation (electrotransformation w E. Coli)

    Reagents:
    Soc Medium1 mL per 1 uL cells
    DNA~ 250 ng

    Protocol:

    1. Using electrocomp cells, let cells thaw on ice (a few minutes, do not leave out for longer than necessary). Keep cuvette and cuvette holder on ice as well.
    2. Add DNA to cells once thawed. Need ~ 250 ng for transformation. Do not incubate.
    3. Add cells to slit in cuvette, put cuvette in holder and push back into clasp of electroporator. Remember to press down on pulse until beep. Check time constant to make sure transformation successful.
    4. Using sterile technique, add SOC medium to cuvette then immediately pipette draw SOC up with cells. Place in pre-prepared 1.5 mL tube.
    5. Incubate cells in shaking incubator for 1 hr.
    6. Afterwards, spin down at 3000 rpm for 5 min to pellet bacteria.
    7. Take out SOC, but leave ~ 60 - 80 uLs. Resuspend pellet and plate.

    Liquid Cultures

    Reagents:
    Nutrient Broth4 mL
    Antibiotic1:1000 ratio between uL antibiotic and uL broth

    Equipment:

    • Plate of Transformed Colonies
    • Large Tubes in Tube Rack
    • Bunsen Burner (sterile technique throughout)
    • Shaking Incubator

    Protocol:

    1. In front of the flame from the bunsen burner, put the nutrient broth and antibiotic in the large tube
    2. Superior broth has more nutrients than LB and therefore has a higher yield
    3. Antibiotic depends on antibiotic resistance of transformed colonies
    4. Pick up a colony from the plate of colonies by touching the colony with a tip (on pipette)
    5. When transferring the colonies to the large tubes, tilt the large tube so the nutrient broth is closer to the opening
    6. Put the very end of the tip (which touched the plate and has the colony) into the broth
    7. Do not dip the entire tip in the nutrient broth because the tip is not that sterile
    8. Shake the tip a little to transfer the colony into the nutrient broth
    9. Take the very end of the tip out of the tube, discard the tube, and cap the tube
    10. Put the tubes in a shaking incubator overnight
    11. Can either miniprep immediately or freeze culture tubes to miniprep later

    Freezing Liquid Culture Tubes

    1. Take liquid culture tubes out of the shaking incubator and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 minutes
    2. Dump the supernatant (liquid) into a beaker
    3. Add 10% bleach to the beaker
    4. Place the large tubes in the freezer covered with plastic wrap
    5. Take out the large tubes only when going to do the miniprep immediately

    Miniprep

    Purpose: To purify plasmid from cells, usually with the liquid cultures created from a transformation

    Reagents
    Plasmid Resuspension Buffer (B1)400 µL
    Plasmid Lysis Buffer (B2)400 µL
    Plasmid Neutralisation Buffer (B3)800 µL
    Plasmid Wash Buffer 1200 µL
    Plasmid Wash Buffer 2400 µL
    DNA Elution Buffer40 µL

    Equipment:

    Protocol:

    1. Pellet liquid culture in 1.5 mL microcentrifuge tube by centrifugation at 13000 rpm for 5-10 minutes, and discard the supernatant
    2. Pipette the liquid culture into a labelled microcentrifuge tube 1 mL at a time
    3. Centrifuge the 1 mL-filled tubes and discard the supernatant
    4. Repeat this process, putting 1 more mL of the liquid culture into the same microcentrifuge tube, until the liquid culture tube is empty
    5. Pellet should form inside the microcentrifuge tube
    6. Resuspend the plasmid in 400 µL Plasmid Resuspension Buffer (B1)
    7. Vortex or pipette until there are no clumps present
    8. Add 400 µL Plasmid Lysis Buffer (B2), and gently invert the tube 5-6 times
    9. Incubate at room temperature for 1 minute
    10. Add 800 µL Plasmid Neutralisation Buffer (B3), and invert the tube until the entire solution is completely yellow
    11. Plasmid Neutralisation Buffer (B3) should be stored in the 4 C freezer
    12. Incubate at room temperature for 2 minutes
    13. Centrifuge at 13000 rpm for 10 minutes
    14. Transfer the supernatant into the spin column (with the collection tube)
    15. Centrifuge at 13000 rpm for 1 minute, and discard the flow-through (in the collection tube)
    16. Add 200 µL Plasmid Wash Buffer 1 to the spin column
    17. Centrifuge at 13000 rpm for 1 minute, and discard the flow-through (in the collection tube)
    18. Add 400 µL Plasmid Wash Buffer 2 to the spin column
    19. Centrifuge at 13000 rpm for 1 minute, and discard the collection tube with the flow-through
    20. If there is any doubt, centrifuge at 13000 rpm for 1 more minute before discarding the collection tube with the flow-through
    21. Do not touch the spin column to the flow-through in the collection tube when removing the spin column
    22. Transfer the spin column to a clean microcentrifuge tube
    23. Add >= 30 µL Elution Buffer
    24. Note: Using more elution buffer will increase overall yield of DNA but will result in final solution of eluted DNA being less concentrated.
    25. Tip: To increase the yield, use 20 uL of Michelle’s Zymo Elution Buffer instead
    26. Tip: To decrease chemical contamination from ethanol, after step 13 spin the tubes with the filter for 1 min at 13,000 rpm without the lid in order to evaporate ethanol from the filters
    27. Tip: To increase yield heat elution buffer using a heating block to 60℃
    28. Incubate at room temperature for 1 minute
    29. Centrifuge at 13000 rpm for 1 minute
    30. Resulting flow-through in the microcentrifuge tube is the purified DNA

    (Based on Monarch Miniprep protocol with revisions from Michelle Byrom)

    Glycerol Stocks

    Reagents:
    Liquid Culture500 uL for 1 stock
    50% Glycerol500 uL for 1 stock

    Protocol:

    1. Inoculate liquid cultures using the standard protocol for liquid cultures and leave overnight or until there is bacterial growth.
    2. Using sterile procedure. add 500 µL of the liquid culture and 500 µL of 50% glycerol to a 1.5 mL microcentrifuge tube. Close lid and gently mix by inverting the tube or vortexing to ensure that the culture and glycerol are not separated.
    3. Stock doesn’t always need to consist of 500 uL liquid culture and 500 uL glycerol, but liquid culture and glycerol must be in a 1:1 ratio.
    4. Put the glycerol stock in the -80℃ freezer to freeze. Glycerol stock should be stable for years so long as it is left at -80℃. Subsequent thaw and freeze cycles will reduce shelf life.
    5. To recover bacteria from a glycerol stock open the top and use a sterile hoop to scrape some frozen bacteria off the top. Streak onto a plate containing the appropriate antibiotic.
    6. Grow on plates overnight at 37℃ (or a lower temperature like 30℃ if a slower growth rate is required).

    Production of L-DOPA*

    Reagents:
    Liquid Cultures of Production Assembly and Controls100 uL
    NF H2ODependent on objective.
    Pre-mixed Nutrient Powder (LB)Dependent on objective.
    TyrosineEnough to make 1 mM and 5 mM solution.

    Protocol:

    1. Make 3 different batches of LB Broth: plain LB, LB with 1 mM tyrosine, and LB with 5 mM tyrosine.
    2. Weigh out enough tyrosine to make 1 mM and 5 mM solution.
    3. Use standard LB protocol to make all three, with exception of adding tyrosine.
    4. Use different batches of LB to make liquid culture stocks with production assembly as well as a control, another plasmid that doesn’t produce L-DOPA.
    5. Use already grown liquid cultures. Take 100 uL grown culture and add to 4 mL of LB/LB w 1mM tyrosine/LB w 5 mM tyrosine.
    6. Remember antibiotic.
    7. Make liquid cultures in triplicates.
    8. Leave new culture tubes in shaking incubator.
    9. Let cultures sit until turn dark (brown color).

    Sensing and Reporting Dopamine*

    Reagents:
    LB BrothDependent on objective.
    DopamineEnough to make 10 mM stock solution.
    L-Ascorbic Acid1 mg/uL solution.
    Liquid Cultures of Sensing Assembly and Controls100 uL
    IPTGEnough for 1 mM solution.

    Protocol:

    1. First make 10 mM DOPA stock solution in order to make other test conditions.
    2. Calculate mLs of 10 mM DOPA stock solution needed. Using sterile technique, add however many mLs of LB needed (for example, if 20 mLs of stock solution needed, add 20 mL LB and base calculations off of that) to a tube.
    3. Add enough dopamine to make 10 mM solution (continuing off example of 20 mL solution, add 0.0363 g of dopamine to make 10 mM solution).
    4. Add ascorbic acid in concentration of 1 mg/uL solution.
    5. Once all added, vortex tube for ~15 - 20 min until dopamine goes into solution.
    6. Sterilize solution by using a syringe and filter.
    7. After having made 10 mM DOPA stock solution, make a 1:10 dilution of 10 mM stock solution to make a 1 mM solution. (If 20 mL of 10 mM stock solution, dilute 2 mL of 10 mM solution in 18 mL LB to make 20 mL of 1 mM DOPA solution). Remember to add ascorbic acid to new stock solution. Then sterilize like before.
    8. Repeat this step until there is 10 mM, 1 mM, 100 uM, 10 uM, and 1 uM DOPA solution.
    9. Use 1 mM, 100 uM, 10 uM, and 1 uM DOPA solution to make liquid cultures from already grown liquid cultures of sensing assembly and controls.
    10. Make 1:50 dilutions of already grown liquid cultures with different media conditions. For example, for 4 mL culture, add 80 uL of already grown culture to 4 mL of LB or a certain DOPA solution.
    11. Do triplicates for each condition for each assembly/control tested.
    12. Remember to make two identical sets, one without IPTG and one with.
    13. Grow for ~ 6 hrs.
    14. When cell OD reaches .6, add 4 uL (1:10 of media) of 1 mM IPTG (after ~3 hours)
    15. Put cultures back into the shaking incubator
    16. Spin down cultures
    17. ~3000 rpm for 5 minutes
    18. Pour out supernatant
    19. Resuspend in 1x Buffer
    20. 4 mL of 1x PBS
    21. Pipette or vortex to resuspend cells
    22. Plate reading
    23. Take 100 uL of the resuspended cells
    24. Load into a 96 well transparent plate
    25. Measure absorbance and fluorescence with Venus assay (excitation: 515 nm, emission: 528 nm)
