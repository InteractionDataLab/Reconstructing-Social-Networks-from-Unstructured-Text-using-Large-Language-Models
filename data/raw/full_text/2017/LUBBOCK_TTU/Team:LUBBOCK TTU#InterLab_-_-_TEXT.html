The iGEM Measurement Committee aims to develop robust measurement procedures for enhancing the quantification and reproducibility of results through the InterLab Study. These results can then be used to reliably characterize biological constructs. For the InterLab Study this year, participating iGEM teams follow a standardized protocol for measuring green fluorescent protein (GFP) using E. coli DH5 cells and a plate reader. Fluorescence data is typically difficult to compare between laboratories because fluorescent measurements are not reported in the same units or the methods of data processing are different. The goal of the InterLab Study is to determine absolute units for measuring GFP that are comparable despite the type of plate reader used to make the measurements. More details about the standardized protocol we used for the study can be found here. Below you will find our data we collected for the InterLab Study using BioTek Synergy 4 plate reader. The InterLab Study can be broken down into two parts: plate reader calibration and cell measurement. To obtain the correction factor we prepared a reference OD600 with a spectrophotometer and collect absorbance data with a plate reader using LUDOX-S40 and DI water.
