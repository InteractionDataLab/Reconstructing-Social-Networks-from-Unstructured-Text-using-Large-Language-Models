Péclet Number The Péclet number for a suspension is a dimensionless quantity which can be used to determine the relative strength of the effects of driving forces and thermal diffusion in the motion of a suspended particle in the solvent. In a general sense, it can be defined as, \[P_{e}=\frac{\text{Rate of motion due to external forces}}{\text{Rate of diffusive transport in the medium}}\] In the case of a particle moving through the media having a effective radius \(a\), the Péclet number takes the form[1], $$P_{e}=\frac{\dot{\gamma} a^{2}}{D_{Th}}$$ References [1] Ramaswamy, Sriram. "Issues in the statistical mechanics of steady sedimentation." Advances in Physics 50.3 (2001): 297-341.
