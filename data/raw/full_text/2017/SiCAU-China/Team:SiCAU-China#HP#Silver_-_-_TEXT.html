After which he took us to the pigsty and sheep fold and show us the production of feed, they generally use the antibiotic premix. The addition of antibiotics in different animals, the same animal of different growth periods are different. Commonly used residual detection method is ELISA, but its specificity and sensitivity to be improved, and the reaction takes longer. Second event:Investigation of antibiotic residues and detection in the environment       We learned condition about the use of antibiotics in animal feed by taking an interview with an interviewer and visiting the Food and Drug Administration. Due to low prices of antibiotics, it is a lot of use in the feeding of animals. Add antibiotics to the feed, which can inhibit growth of the pathogens in animals and reduce animal mortality. Therefore, the problems of animal disease complicating and antibiotics residue get more serious. On the one hand, we will spend a lot of energy to treat the difficult animal diseases. Besides, antibiotics residue flow to the environment which cause a large number of resistant bacteria multiply, polluting the surrounding water.       Now, using Liquid Chromatography to detect antibiotics is the main way which the costs are high and taking a long time. We also exchanged our projects with teachers and technical staff at the same time. According to our survey results, the using microbiological detection of antibiotics has great significance, which reducing reliance on precision instruments and detection costs significantly. Third event: An investigation of antibiotic residues       This summer,we designed a signal amplification system using microorganisms to achieve trace detection of antibiotics or other substances. We would like to know the public's attitude towards the detection and prevention of antibiotics. Setting up an online questionnaire help us learn more about antibiotics. From the survey results, we can find that it is necessary to detect antibiotics and we need to do more to strengthen public understanding of antibiotics. The statistical results are as follows: 1. What is your gender? 2. Do you often use antibiotics in your daily life? 3.Do you know the difference between tetracycline antibiotics and cephalosporins antibiotics? 4. Do you think you have had antibiotic abuse? 5. Your understanding of the current antibiotic residue in your daily life? 6. Your views and attitudes about antibiotic residues? 7. Do you know the existing means of antibiotic testing? 8. Do you think traceable substances are easy to detect? 9. How do you think about the use of engineered bacteria (no pathogenicity) to detect antibiotic residues in the environment? 10. Do you want to use this method to detect antibiotic residues such as food?
