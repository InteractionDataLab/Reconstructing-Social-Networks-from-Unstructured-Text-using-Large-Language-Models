The mutation rates in the calculation of the minimal lagoon volume are assumed to be constant over time and the same for every position in the sequence. This basic model does not apply well when highly conserved regions are subject to mutation. However, if the length of conserved regions is substracted from the total sequence length this should lead to more accurate results. About the medium consumption heatmap When working with a turbidostat the upper x axis is relevant, when working with a chemostat the lower is. Put the mouse pointer on the heatmap to show the exact values for that point. You can zoom in by drawing the rectangle that should be shown with pressed left mouse button. You can always save the current plot by clicking on the camera icon. Note Submitting one of the forms recalculates all values and the heatmap based on the values in all the forms. When a high resolution is chosen this may take a moment.
