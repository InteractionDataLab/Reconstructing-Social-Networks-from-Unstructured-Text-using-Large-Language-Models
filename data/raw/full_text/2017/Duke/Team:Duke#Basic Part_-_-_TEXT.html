Using the YASARA and ZDOCK modeling packages, we were able to identify residues with high B factors and predict which amino acid changes were required to improve the stability of folded structure. After changing these amino acids, we simulated the interactions and binding interfaces between our thermoengineered proteins and antibodies and glycoproteins. Through this, we determined that our thermostable variants were predicted to have higher melting temperatures and have preserved binding loops. Please check out our modeling page for more information on the process of thermoengineering!
