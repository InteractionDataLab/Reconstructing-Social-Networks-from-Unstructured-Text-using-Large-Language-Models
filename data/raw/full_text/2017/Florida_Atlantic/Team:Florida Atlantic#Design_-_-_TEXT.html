A sample of a suspect medicine can then be added to a culture of this biosensor to determine its artemisinin content. If artemisinin is not present, or is present in small concentrations—as happens in some substandard medicines—the change in color will be less noticeable than would be seen with authentic artemisinin. Because we do not want the cell walls of E. coli to interfere with the diffusion of either artemisinin or the binding protein, a genetic device that lyses the bacterial cells is also included. It is controlled by an inducible promoter, however, so that the bacteria can produce sufficient protein for artemisinin to be the limiting factor before lysing.
