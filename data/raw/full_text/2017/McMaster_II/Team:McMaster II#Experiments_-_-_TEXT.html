Chill on ice for 2 minutes Add in 600 uL of LB media and incubate at 37°C for 1 hour Plate 200 uL onto LB agar plate with the appropriate antibiotic using a streaker After a minimum of 12 hours, proceed to picking colonies and glycerol stocks protocol Picking Colonies and Glycerol Stocks Procedure: Prepare work area and wipe it down with 70% ethanol Obtain your bacterial plate(s) with the colony of interest on them Using a serological pipette, dispense 5 mL of liquid culture medium (LB) into the snap cap culture tubes. One tube per colony. Then add in 5uL of corresponding antibiotic Label the tubes with the plasmid and colony number Using a pipette tip, gently pick a single colony form the plate and inoculate the corresponding culture tube. Drop the pipette tip in. Cap the tubes to the first stop Rack the culture tubes at an angle in test tube racks, and incubate in a shaking incubator at 37°C and 250 rpm. Incubate for at least 12 hours, but do not exceed 18 hours After overnight bacterial culture growth, add 500 uL of the culture to 500 uL of 50% glycerol in 2 mL cryo-vials and gently mix. Freeze the glycerol stock tube at -80°C Proceed to Mini-Prep protocol after making glycerol stocks Plasmid Mini-Prep Materials GeneAid Miniprep Kit Bacterial Cell Culture Procedure: Spin down overnight cultures at 2000 rpm for 10 minutes Discard supernatant. Do not disturb cell pellet Resuspend in 500 uL of distilled water Transfer to 1.5 mL microcentrifuge tube and spin at 16000*g for 1 minute. Discard supernatant Add 200 uL PD1 Buffer (with RNase A) to the tube and resuspend the pellet by vortexing Add 200 uL PD2 Buffer to the tube. Invert 10 times to gently mix Let stand at room temperature for at least 2 minutes, but do not exceed 5 minutes Add 300 uL PD3 Buffer to the tube. Invert 10 times to mix Centrifuge at 16000*g for 3 minutes Place a PD column in a 2 mL collection tube Carefully transfer the supernatant from Step 9 without transferring any of the white precipitate Centrifuge at 16000*g for 30 seconds and discard the flow-through Add 400 uL W1 Buffer to the tube and centrifuge at 16000*g for 30 seconds. Discard the flow-through Add 600 uL Wash Buffer (with ethanol) to the tube and centrifuge at 16000*g for 30 seconds. Discard the flow-through Centrifuge the column and collection tube at 16000*g for 3 minutes to dry the column Transfer the column into a new 1.5 mL microcentrifuge tube Add 50 uL of ddH2O into the centre of the column matrix Let stand for at least 2 minutes for absorption Centrifuge at 16000*g for 2 minutes to elute the purified DNA. It is helpful to be generous with the extension time Proceed to Agarose Gel Electrophoresis Protocol to analyze results of PCR Agarose Gel Electrophoresis Procedure: Prepare a 100 mL of TAE buffer and add in agarose (concentration from 0.8 – 3% w/v) in a 250 mL flask and mx it thoroughly. Place the flask in the microwave, heat until the solution is completely clear. The agarose concentration depends on the band size of interest (2kb – 10kb → 1%; 1kb – 2kb → 1.5%; 200bp – 1kb → 2%; 40bp – 200bp → 3% When the gel does not burn your hand anymore, add 5 uL of RedSafe Nucleic Staining solution (20000X) to the agarose solution. Swirl the flask gently to mix the solution and avoid forming bubbles (5uL for 100mL, 10uL for 200mL) Pour it into the gel tray until the comb teeth are immersed about ¼ - ½ into the agarose Allow the agarose gel to cool until solidified. This usually takes 20 minutes Add adequate amount of 6X loading dye to DNA samples to have a final concentration of 1X. Load samples on the gel Perform electrophoresis: 120 V for 45 minutes Detect the bands under UV illumination Gel Extraction Procedure: Excise the agarose gel slice containing the relevant DNA fragments. Remove any excess agarose to minimize size of gel slice Transfer up to 300 mg of the gel slice to a 1.5 mL microcentrifuge tube Add 500 uL of DF Buffer to the same and mix by vortex Incubate at 50-60°C for 10-15 minutes to ensure the gel slice is fully dissolved. Invert every 2-3 minutes. Cool the dissolved sample mixture to room temperature Place the DF column in a 2 mL Collection Tube Transfer 800 uL of the sample mixture to the DF column Centrifuge at 16000*g for 30 seconds. Discard flow-through Add 400 uL of W1 Buffer into the DF column Centrifuge at 16000*g for 30 seconds. Discard the flow-through Add 600 uL of Wash Buffer (with ethanol) into the DF column Let stand for 1 minute at room temperature Centrifuge at 16000*g for 30 seconds. Discard the flow-through Centrifuge at 16000*g for 3 minutes to dry the column matrix Transfer the dried DF column to a new 1.5 mL microcentrifuge tube Add 30 uL of ddH2O into the center of the column matrix Let stand for 2 minutes to ensure water is completely absorbed Centrifuge at 16000*g for 2 minutes to elute the purified DNA Use a spectrophotometer to measure the DNA concentration of the purified sample Colony PCR with Taq Mastermix Procedure for Digestion: Determine what digestions need to be done and the enzymes required for these digestions The amount of DNA necessary for digestion is 500 ng.
