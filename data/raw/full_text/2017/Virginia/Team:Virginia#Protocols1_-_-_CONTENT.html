    • Chemical Transformation

    Methods:

    1. Thaw 50µL competent E. coli cells on ice for 10 minutes
    2. Add:
    3. 5-10 µl DNA from a ligation reaction mix or
    4. 10-100ng DNA of a known plasmid
    5. Carefully flick the tube 4-5 times to mix cells and DNA. Do not vortex.
    6. Place the mixture on ice for 30 minutes. Do not mix.
    7. Heat shock at exactly 42°C for exactly 30 seconds. Do not mix.
    8. Place on ice for 5 minutes. Do not mix.
    9. Pipette 950 µl of room temperature SOC or LB media into the mixture.
    10. Incubate at 37°C and 200-250 rpm for 60 minutes.
    11. Mix the cells thoroughly by flicking the tube and inverting.
    12. Spread:
    13. For ligation reaction DNA: 100µl of each transformation reaction onto a selection plate. For the rest of 900 µL:
    14. Pellet cells at 8000rpm for 3 minutes
    15. Remove and dispense 600 µL of supernatant
    16. Re-suspend cells by light vortexing
    17. Plate resuspended cells as above
  • For known plasmid: 10 & 100 µL of each transformation reaction onto a selection plate. For the rest of 890 µL:
  • Pellet cells at 8000rpm for 3 minutes
  • Remove and dispense 600 µL of supernatant
  • Re-suspend cells by light vortexing
  • Plate resuspended cells as above
  • Incubate overnight at 37°C with plates upside down.
