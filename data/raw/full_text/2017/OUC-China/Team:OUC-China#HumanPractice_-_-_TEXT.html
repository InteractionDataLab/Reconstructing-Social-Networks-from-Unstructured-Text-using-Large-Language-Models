This year our project was inspired by our local environmental problem in Qingdao, which located along the coastline of Yellow Sea in China. From June to August every year, a large quantity of floating Enteromorpha appeared offshore in Qingdao. Every year, the government will fund the disposal of Enteromorpha. Statistics show that Enteromorpha's annual salvage can reach up to 70000 tons. Still, a huge amount of Enteromorpha was not utilized fully after salvage, which mainly turned into fodder. Therefore, our goal is to find a better way to maximize the utilization of seaweed residue. Meanwhile, we are concerned with the world energy crisis, in which bioenergy is one of the main renewable energy sources of human development that can be of great help. Therefore, we hope to transform the algae fiber into bioenergy and make a modest effort to help deal with the world energy crisis considering the overgrown Enteromorpha. Inheriting the spirits of iGEM Organizing Committee, our project comes from the social environmental problem and would give feedback to society in the way of energy producing and education. That is just how society affects our work and how our work affects society. We have done a full human practice this year. It can be summed up as three perspectives, inspired by society, contacts with society and feedback to society as well as a communication and cooperation section. Fig 2.
