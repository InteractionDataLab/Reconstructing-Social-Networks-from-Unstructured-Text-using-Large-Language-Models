Chris Today, I helped Emily make a massive PCR with varying concentrations of random stuff in order to bring MalEΔSS and MalE31ΔSS out of the plasmids sent to us by Jean-Michel Betton's lab. The different PCRs included gradients of annealing tempeature, [MgCl2, varying primer concentrations, varying quantities of DNA, and using Pfx Polymerase with MgSO4 as the salt. This resulted in 120 wells of PCR product that could then be inserted into Biobrick vectors.
