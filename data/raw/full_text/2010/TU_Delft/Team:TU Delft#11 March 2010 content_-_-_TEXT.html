In total we have got 9 interested students, 2 instructors and 2 advisers. We started by introducing ourselves and talked about what we thought were the most interesting past iGEM projects. It turned out that we really liked the ideas concerning a sensor of some sort, which was coupled to an output (to put in very generally). Furthermore we discussed some does and don'ts for an iGEM project, such that it would be smartest to have and idea that can be split up into parts, and we made plans for a next meeting, which will be out first brainstorming session.
