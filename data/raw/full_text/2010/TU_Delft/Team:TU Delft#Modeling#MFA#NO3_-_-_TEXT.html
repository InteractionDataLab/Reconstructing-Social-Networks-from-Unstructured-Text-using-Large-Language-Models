The oxygen is used for the oxidative phosphorylation, regenerating NADH, and for the first step in the hydrocarbon degradation. To be more efficient with oxygen an additional electron acceptor was introduced. In this scenario the oxidative phosphorylation was disabled and a replacement was implemented; NO3- + NADH + 8 H+ -> NO2- + NAD+ + H+ + 4 Hex+ + H2O This reaction uses NO3 as an electron acceptor to regenerate NADH and export protons to generate ATP. Less protons are exported per mol of NADH, so the ATP/NADH ratio will drop compared to oxygen.
