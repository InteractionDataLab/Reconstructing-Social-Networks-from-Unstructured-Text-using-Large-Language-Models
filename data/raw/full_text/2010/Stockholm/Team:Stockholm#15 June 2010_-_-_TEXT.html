The plate with our competent cells but without control-plasmid DNA had no colonies, confirming that the ampicillin worked in our plates (even though they were left overnight in normal lab room), as otherwise other bacteria would also be able to grow on the plates. Colonies had been made on monday, with SOD, bFGF and IgG-protease, using the commercial cells. SOD and bFGF had very many colonies, both the normal plate and the 10x dilution plate. IgG-protease did not have any colonies (other antibiotic?). Then I did an overnight culture. For SOD and bFGF: 12 ml LB and 24 ¬µl (50 mg/ml) Amp added to a 50 ml Falcon tube, then one colony was added.
