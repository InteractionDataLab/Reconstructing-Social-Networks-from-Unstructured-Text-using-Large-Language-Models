    Tutorial - Sin operon

    1. Evolutionary design of a stochastic switch for synthetic genetic circuits
    2. Sin operon - Control of sporulation/cell phasing/biofilm/ flaggella formation
    3. SinI/SinR
    4. Components of biofilm (SinR tetramer inhibits biofilm formation, activates flaggellum formation)
    5. SinI reduces rate of tetramer formation by forming a dimer with SinR.
    6. S08
    7. Cytoscape - drawing networks
    8. Modelling Stochastic switch
    9. Voigt + Kobashyi model
    10. Run simulation - does it form a biofilm or flagellum? Develop switch that would predict correct 60 % of the time.
    11. Pull out kinetic parameters from literature.
    12. Cell designer. Capasi - no graphical interface, but more powerful. Can write Java code and call Capasi from it more specific activities. SBML shorthand. Differential equations to SBML

    Research Feedback

    1. Properties of concrete and surface tension (Zoltan and Steven)

    8. Jen confirmed that will need to to plasmid work in E.coli first because it will not be stable in Bacillus. As long as use correct vector, should work in Bacillus though.

    10. Sporulation, germination and secretion control (Alan and Da)

Jem: additional system - to pick up on subtilin signal rather than engineering into the Sin system and causing unexpected effects. (Gold award: improving an existing biobrick).
    13. Kill switch (Richard and Jannetta)
    14. Linear DNA (Richard)
    15. Produced model for exonucleases, structures resistant to these and their disruption by 5’ gap produces by DNA replication. To produce model for simple eating away by exonucleases.
