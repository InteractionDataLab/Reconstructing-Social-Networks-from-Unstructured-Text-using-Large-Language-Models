    • Restriction Digest protocol

    Brown iGEM 2010, modified from Knight Lab protocol.

    Materials

    • Restriction enzymes (we use NEB)
    • NEB buffer 2/3 (use buffer finder on NEB website. Buffer 2 is standard for iGEM enzymes)
    • BSA
    • Deionized, sterile H2O

    Digest mix

    The reaction below is a 50ul reaction, 100ul reactions are also common if you want your DNA to be dilute.

    • 5ul NEB Buffer 2
    • Xul DNA (usually ~500ng)
    • 0.5ul 100x BSA
    • 1ul Restriction Enzyme (regardless of the reaction, 1ul is used because this generally represents a 10-25 fold excess. It is also sticky due to its glycerol content, so pipetting can be difficult)
    • 1ul Restriction Enzyme 2 (for double digests)
    • (42.5-X)ul dH2O

    Protocol

    1. Add appropriate amount of deionized H2O to sterile 0.6 mL tube
    2. Add restriction enzyme buffer to the tube.
    3. Vortex buffer before pipetting to ensure that it is well-mixed.
    4. Add BSA to the tube.
    5. Vortex BSA before pipetting to ensure that it is well-mixed.
    6. Add appropriate amount of DNA to be cut to the tube.
    7. Vortex DNA before pipetting to ensure that it is well-mixed.
    8. Add 1 μL of each enzyme.
    9. Vortex enzyme before pipetting to ensure that it is well-mixed.
    10. Also, the enzyme is in some percentage of glycerol which tends to stick to the sides of your tip. To ensure you add only 1 μL, just touch your tip to the surface of the liquid when pipetting.
    11. Place in thermal cycler (MJ Research, PT-200) and run digest protocol.
    12. 4-6 hour incubation at 37°C
    13. Use a longer incubation time if you have time or are worried about the efficiency of cutting. I think this time can be shortened to 2 hrs while still cutting to completion.
    14. 20 mins at 80°C to heat inactivate enzyme.
    15. This step is sufficient to inactivate even Pst I.
    16. 4°C forever (or until you pull the reaction out of the thermal cycler).
  • Generally, use some method of DNA purification to eliminate enzymes and salt from the reaction.
