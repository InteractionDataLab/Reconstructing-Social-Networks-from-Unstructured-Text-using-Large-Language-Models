May Week 1: 1-4 may a) It was made at 7 Gibson parts, to obtain finally the Working Plasmid (see section constructs). b) Transform E. coli and then in culture plates (LB + KanR), we expect to grow overnight. c) There are red plates; we don’t expect that, we think is a contamination. So again we held the Gibson plasmid construct working (on two different plates). In one observed 2 colonies grow in the other about 10 (:D!). d) In the wake of the red colonies, we believe that Gibson could not have come out quite right. To ensure that our construct was made in good way, we construct colony PCR assembly (with / without taking into account the vector backbone). When you run the gel, we realized that our construct actually corresponded to the expected product (hence the Gibson worked OK, :D). e) As our work Gibson, Gibson performed another to generate our second and third construct (HIV protease sfGFP reporter and producer, respectively). f) Competent bacteria were transformed with the products of previous Gibson.
