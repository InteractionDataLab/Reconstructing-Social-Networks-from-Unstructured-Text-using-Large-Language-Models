            Journal
            • Apr 17th, 2012

              • YAO. Genome Seminar: Papers from Yu CHEN should be read CAREFULLY which are mainly about plasmid introduction to mitochondrial.

              Problems to be solved:

              1. Experiment principle;

              2. Why necessary to each step;

              3. Bacterial strain to be used;

              4. Culture to be used.

              Core Papers:

              1. Fox et al. SCIENCE, VOL. 240. Mitochondrial Transformation in Yeast by Bombardment with Microprojectiles.

              2. STEPHEN A. JOHNSTON et al. Proc. Natl. Acad. Sci. USA 85 (1988). Plasmids can stably transform yeast mitochondria lacking endogenous mtDNA

              Participants:

              Junguang XU, Qiwu XU, Haojun JIANG, Jie WANG, Qiong WU, Senwei TANG, Qinsi LIANG, Min OU.

            • Apr 20th, 2012

              • YAO. Genome experiment design for first part:

              Task assignment:

              1. Usage of Gene gun and fluorescence microscope

              2. Research on mitochondria transformation methods

              3. Research on Saccharomyces cerevisiae genome, especially transcription system.

              4. Research on plasmid transformation directly or after isolated mitochondria.

              5. Research on detection methods about whether transformation is succeed.

              Core paper:

              Nathalie Bonnefoy et al. METHODS IN CELL BIOLOGY, VOL. 80. Genetic Transformation of Saccharomyces cerevisiae and Chlamydomonas reinhardtii Mitochondria.

              Participants:

              Junguang XU, Qiwu XU, Haojun JIANG, Jie WANG, Qiong WU, Senwei TANG, Qinsi LIANG, Min OU.
