The four modules of the genetic circuit level approach are: Arsenic transport – this will be a model that describes the movement of arsenic across the cell membrane, which takes into account the kinetic constants of the interaction with the arsenic transporter. Arsenic accumulation – this model will describe the sequestration of arsenic inside the cell as a result of the interaction with the proteins ArsR and the metalothionein. Biosensor – a classical switch-like model that produces a reporter protein in direct proportion to the concentration of an input, which in this case is arsenic. Silica binding – a model that describes the interaction between the membrane-embedded chimeric protein with the silica binding domain and the silica particles.
