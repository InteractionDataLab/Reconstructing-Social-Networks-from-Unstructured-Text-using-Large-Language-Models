We would expect to see the sudden increase in expression of the reporter gene, in this case, mRFP. Before start, we incubate the E. coli with promoter Phs and mRFP as a reporter at room temperature 25 Celsius degree. We transient shift the E. coli to a higher temperature ambient (37 Celsius degree), and closely monitor the expression of the reporter. As we can see in the figure, the promoter responds to the temperature changes pretty quick and after 30 minutes, it show 6 fold augmentation in mRFP emission (wavelength 610 nm). We continue the test and tendency of increment shows no sign of subsidence.
