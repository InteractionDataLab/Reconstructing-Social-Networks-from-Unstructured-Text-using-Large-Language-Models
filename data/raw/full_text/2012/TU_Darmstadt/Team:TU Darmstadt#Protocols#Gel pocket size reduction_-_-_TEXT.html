This would usually result in pouring a new gel, but what if there is not enough agarose left or one is in a hurry? We improvised a method for gel pocket size reduction, making it possible to split one pocket into two. Required tools Agarose gel some excess agarose (less than 20µl worked in our case) a ruler or any other improvised support a mauly a pipet tip Instruction Cut of a piece of the pipet tip so that it does fit smoothly in the gel pocket Fix the upper section of the tip on the ruler/support using the mauly Put the construction on your gel puring chamber (already filled (isert the tip in the pocket at 3/4 of its width)) Add a small volume of agar solution to the side of the tip with more space and let the agar dry (~3 min) Remove the tip an use the gel Retrieved from " http://2012.igem.org/Team:TU_Darmstadt/Protocols/Gel_pocket_size_reduction " Recent changes What links here Related changes Special pages My preferences Printable version Permanent link Privacy policy Disclaimers
