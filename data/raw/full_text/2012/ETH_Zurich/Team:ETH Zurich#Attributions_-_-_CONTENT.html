    • Attributions

    Our Advisors helped us with their broad knowledge of different fields to finalizes the idea of our project. During our project they supported us in the design of our experiments and with useful discussions of our results.

    The work in the lab as well as the modeling part was fulfilled by the team members only.

    Acknowledgements

    Daniel Gerngross Photography

    We need to thank the following people and labs for their kind help and support for our project. We want to thank

    • Daniel Gerngross responsible for logo design and taking group pictures and the team picture.
    • Prof. Peter Kast, ETH Zurich for providing the E.coli strain K12.
    • Roman Ulm, University of Geneva for providing the cDNA of UVR8 from Arabidopsis thaliana.
    • Jeff Tabor, Rice University (Houston) for providing the JT2-strain and the pJT122 and pJT118 plasmids.
    • Andreas Bosshart from Prof. Panke group at BSSE, ETH Zurich for providing pSEVA-LacI plasmid (pSEVA183 derived plasmid).
    • Christian Mayer from Prof. Stelling group at BSSE, ETH Zurich for letting us work with his software Ru2ren (rules to reaction network)
    • Fabian Rudolf from Prof. Stelling group at BSSE, ETH Zurich for valuable discussions on TetRDBD-UVR8 topic.
    • Gaspar Morgado from Prof. Panke group at BSSE, ETH Zürich for his valuable advice considering operator regions and the design of the hybrid promoters.
    • We want to thank Prof. Sven Panke and Prof. Joerg Stelling for all the helpful discussion and financial support.
    • As an iGEM veterane Moritz was a great help in discussing our modeling approaches.
    • We want to thank Johannes and Markus for holding up with our constant discussions of experimental set-ups.
    • We also want to thank Verena for her support using the FACS machine.
    • Prof. Ingo Potrykus and Mr. Markus Ritter for their time answering our questions for the human practice.
    • We thank the iGEM Team Uppsala 2012 for providing their low copy plasmids and chromoproteins.
