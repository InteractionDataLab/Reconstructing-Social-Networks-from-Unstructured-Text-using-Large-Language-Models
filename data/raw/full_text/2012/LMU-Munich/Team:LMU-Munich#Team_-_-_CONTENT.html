    Who we are

    Our iGEM team is a group of seven students and three mentors.

    As we are a team of only ten people, and the iGEM competition is a new experience for many of us, we work closely together on all aspects of our work. There is no graph, no abstract, not even a single presentation slide that isn't enthusiastically discussed by the whole team. We divide our lab work as well, so that each of us has spent some time working on the other modules.

    All of us are working closely together with our two instructors Prof. Thorsten Mascher and Dr. Tina Wecke and our advisor Dr. Georg Fritz. Prof. Thorsten Mascher is not only offering us a place in his lab, but also a lot of support and input on all parts of the project. Tina is the one to go to for trouble shooting and strategy planning, and Georg is advising us how to best evaluate parts and how to model.

    We have a lot of fun working together on Beadzillus!

    Where we are from

    München mag dich!

    This is the slogan of Munich, the city where our team is from. It stands for the cosmopolitan character of Munich. But it is also a declaration of love to its citizens. All of us enjoy the variety of activities that Munich has to offer. The inner city shows traces of Munich|s history, the English Garden provides the space for sunny or snowy days, quarters are complete with diverse museums, galleries, cafes and bars... In many of these quarters you will meet many students, on the run or relaxing between classes, as two universities are located in Munich, Technical University of Munich (TUM) and Ludwig-Maximilians-University Munich (LMU). We study at the LMU, which is famous for over 500 years of research in academics. We comprise the LMU's third iGEM team. Working hard in the lab, we strive to make the LMU, and the city of Munich, proud of our accomplishments in this year's competition.
