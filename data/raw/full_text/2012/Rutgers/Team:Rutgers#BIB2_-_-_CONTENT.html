    Biofuels in Biology

    To develop strains of bacteria that produce high levels of 1-butanol we have introduced the genes coding for a biochemical pathway from Clostridium acetobutylicum into a mutant E. coli strain that produces a high level of NADH.The combination of these chemical pathways is predicted to increase the level of butanol production.. >>Read more...Plasmid MapResults

    The Etch-a-Sketch project aims to create a lawn of bacteria that can be drawn on with a laser pointer. This seemingly inconsequential task actually presents many interesting engineering challenges. We have designed a novel genetic switch to tackle these problems. If our work proves successful, it will serve as a useful model for future projects that require large signal amplification. Read more...

    Genetic Circuit and Organization

    We have organized our genetic circuit in three plasmids:

    • Plasmid 1 - pBT330-Bu2 – phaA, hbd, crt
    • Plasmid 2 - pCWOri-ter.adhE2 – adhE2, ter, fdh
    • Plasmid 3 - pBBR1-aceEF.lpd – aceEF-lpd operon

    Plasmid 1 - pBT330-Bu2 – phaA, hbd, crt

    phaA (Crotonyl-CoA -> Butyryl-CoA) Organism: R. eutrophus Amino Acids: 393         Nucleotides: 1182http://www.kegg.jp/dbget-bin/www_bget?reh:H16_A1438

    hbd (Acetoacetyl-CoA -> 3-hydroxybutyryl) Organism: C. acetobutylicum Amino Acids: 282 Nucleotides: 849http://www.genome.jp/dbget-bin/www_bget?cac:CA_C2708

    crt (3-hydroxybutyrl -> Crotonyl-CoA) Organism: C. acetobutylicum Amino Acids: 261 Nucleotides: 786http://www.genome.jp/dbget-bin/www_bget?cac:CA_C2712

    Plasmid 2 - pCWOri-ter.adhE2 – adhE2, ter, fdh

    adhE2 (Butyryl-CoA -> Butylaidehyde -> 1-butanol) Organism: C. acetobutylicum Amino Acids: 858 Nucleotides: 2,574 http://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/Q9ANR5

    ter (Crotonyl-CoA -> Butyryl-CoA) -- full name: (trans-2-enoyl-CoA reductase) Organism: Treponema denticola Amino Acids: 397 Nucleotides: 1194http://www.genome.jp/dbget-bin/www_bget?tde:TDE0597

    fdh Organism: Candida boidinii Amino Acids: 364 Nucleotides: 1,092

     

    Plasmid 3 - pBBR1-aceEF.lpd – aceEF-lpd operon

    pBBR1-aceEF.lpd – aceEF-lpd operon   Organism: DH10B Operon: aceEF-lpdhttp://www.biocyc.org/ECOL316385/NEW-IMAGE?type=OPERON&object=TUH9B-53

    aceE aceF The aceE coding region of DNA codes for a protein that operates as a pyruvate dyhydrogenase.  This plays a key role in the beginning of the pathway, as the pyruvate is broken down into Acetyl-CoA.  The aceF coding region of DNA codes for a protein that acts as a dihydrolipoyl transacetylase, which is also responsible for pyruvate decarboxylation which links glycolysis to the citric acid cycle.  

    aceE (pyruvate dehydrogenase subunit) Organism: DH10B Amino Acid: 887 Nucleotides: 2664http://www.genome.jp/dbget-bin/www_bget?ecd:ECDH10B_0094 Note: this gene has EcoRI site, needs to be optimized

    aceF (dihydrollpoamide acetyltransferase) Amino Acids: 630 Nucleotides: 1893http://www.genome.jp/dbget-bin/www_bget?ecd:ECDH10B_0095

    lpd (dihydrollpoamide dehydrogenase) Amino Acids: 474 Nucleotides: 1425http://www.genome.jp/dbget-bin/www_bget?ecd:ECDH10B_0096
