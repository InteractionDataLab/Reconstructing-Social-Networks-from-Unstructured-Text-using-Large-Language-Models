Composition of the team : > Students > Advisors > Instructors A multidisciplinary and complementary team The members of the team come from different formations: - Evry university, Master mSSB (Synthetic and systemic biology) - Versailles Saint Quentin university - Paris Pantheon university (Philosophy) - Ecole Normale Supérieure - Ecole Centrale Paris (Engineering) - Sup'Biotech Paris (Biotechnology) - ESIEE Management (Biotechnology) - Télécom Sud Paris (Computer Science & managment) - Epita (Computer Science) Our environment: a key location for synthetic biology in France The Institute of Systems and Synthetic Biology (iSSB): iSSB is located on the Genopole® in Evry, host team in the summer. The iSSB is a laboratory at the University of Evry and CNRS, supported by Genopole®. It is a multidisciplinary environment or collaborate physicists, chemists, computer scientists and biologists. It is also the laboratory who founded and directs the Master 2 Systems Biology Synthetic and MSSB. This master of avant-garde, unique in France, offers courses provided by researchers at the forefront of their field, which guaranteed training in the state of the art techniques used in synthetic biology. Alfonso Jaramillo Contact Team director of the Bio-Synth of iSSB After a doctorate in theoretical physics, he was converted into synthetic biology for over 10 years. He gained international recognition in this field. Dr Andrew Tolonen Contact Researcher at Genoscope in Evry After a PhD in genetics and genomics of cyanobacteria at MIT and a post-doc in the team of George Church at Harvard, one of the largest synthetic biology laboratories in the world, he is now a researcher at Genoscope in Evry. His work focuses on the manufacture of biofuels by cyanobacteria. Andrew Tolonen attends the iGEM team Evry-Genopole in the selection and construction of the project. He participated in the supervision of Harvard iGEM teams during his post-doctorate. Thomas Landrain Contact Doctoral student at iSSB Ex-student of the Ecole Normale Superieure in Paris, he is now a doctoral thesis at iSSB where he developed new technologies, based on the dynamics of RNA, for re-programming and display of gene expression in bacteria.
