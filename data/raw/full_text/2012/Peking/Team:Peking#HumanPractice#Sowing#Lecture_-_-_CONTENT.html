    Life as Machines--- Rational Design for Artificial Biological Systems

    On July 14th, with the help of the Beijing Teenager Science & Technology Club, we gave a series of lectures under the theme of "Life as Machines --- Rational Design for Artificial Biological Systems". We presented basic concepts and research methods and techniques of synthetic biology to these students, and introduced the affairs of the iGEM competition to them. Hundreds of students attended this lecture and our team members were bombarded with questions afterwards.

    Figure 1. Lecture "Life as Machines --- Rational Design for Artificial Biological Systems".

    The first lecture, "Introduction to Synthetic Biology" was given by one of our team members, Xing Wenmin. At the beginning of the lecture, Wenmin asked a question, "What is biology in your mind?", which strongly captured students' imagination and then led to our theme --- Life as Machines. This introductory lecture covered some fancy work of synthetic biology over the years, such as the colorful and aromatic E. coli, as well as fundamental biological knowledge necessary for better understanding of synthetic biology, such as the application of E. coli in the laboratory, central dogma, gene regulation and biosensors.

    if you cannot view this video，please click HERE.

    The second lecture, "Research Guidelines of Synthetic Biology" was given by Xiong Hongyu, our deputy team leader. He used an analogy to compare research guidelines of synthetic biology to the engineering cycles, and used a typical project, "A synthetic biological approach to solve heavy metal contamination", which was Peking 2010's iGEM project, as a demonstration. The lecture exhibited research guidelines as "specification --- design --- modeling --- test --- production" and revealed that the researching approaches have combined methods in multiple subjects such as physics, biology, and chemistry.

    if you cannot view this video，please click HERE.

    Then a sharing session was held to provide a chance for these secondary school students to ask any questions that had sprouted in their minds with all this newfound knowledge in synthetic biology and iGEM, and also to share any opinions they had. For example, they expressed major concerns about GMOs around them and asked about suspected GMO-induced health problems that had been reported. Such questions were analysed and further discussed; thus, a more scientifically-based and alert vigilance was established among lecture attendees.

    Figure 2. Students are seriously listening to the lectures.

    Figure 3. Students are discussing the topics covered in the lectures.

    The lectures turned to be a huge success. Afterwards, many of the students approached our team members and actively asked for suggestions about forming their own iGEM teams, and for guidance in their experiments as well. In fact, students from Beijing No.4 High School and The Second High School attached to Beijing Normal University had shown they were more than willing to form a team to take part in the coming iGEM high school competition in 2013. The splendor of this occasion was recorded by the club and was put on its official website Beijing Teenager Science & Technology Club.

    Reflection
