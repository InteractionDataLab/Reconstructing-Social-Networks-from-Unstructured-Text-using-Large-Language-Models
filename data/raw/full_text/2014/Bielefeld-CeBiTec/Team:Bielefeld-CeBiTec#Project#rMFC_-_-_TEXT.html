We want to use electricity to chemically reduce these mediators and transport them into the cells. The process takes place in a bioreactor called "reverse microbial fuel cell" (rMFC). One important requirement for a suitable mediator is that its reduction potential is high enough to restore reduction equivalents, like NAD(P)H (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide (phosphate)). These reduction equivalents enter the respiratory chain where ATP (adenosine triphosphate) is produced which will be used in the next module. Here you will find the results of the rMFC.
