  1. Mix 150 ng of the backbone and equimolar amounts of the insert(s) in water in a PCR tube for a total volume of 15 Âµl.
  2. Add 1.5 Âµl of 10X T4 Ligase Buffer and, when using BsaI or another restriction enzyme that requires it, BSA (Bovine Serum Albumin) at a final concentration of 1X.
  3. Add 1 Âµl of each restriction enzyme and 1 Âµl of T4 DNA Ligase (400,000 cohesive end ligation units/ml).

Gel Electrophoresis

  1. Measure out 7.5 g of agarose.
  2. Pour agarose powder into microwavable flask along with 500mL of 1xTAE.
  3. Microwave for 1-3min (until the agarose is completely dissolved and there is a nice rolling boil).
  4. Let agarose solution cool down for 5min.
  5. Add 10 Âµl of 10 mg/l ethidium bromide (EtBr) stock solution (final concentration 0.2 mg/l). EtBr binds to the DNA and allows you to visualize the DNA under ultraviolet (UV) light.
  6. Pour the agarose into a gel tray with the well comb in place.
  7. Place newly poured gel at 4Â°C for 10-15 minutes OR let sit at room temperature for 20-30 minutes, until it has completely solidified.
  8. Add loading buffer to each of your digest samples.
  9. Once solidified, place the agarose gel into the gel box (electrophoresis unit).
  10. Fill gel box with 1xTAE until the gel is covered.
  11. Carefully load your samples into the additional wells of the gel (recommended: 15 Âµl).
  12. Run the gel at 80-150 V until the dye line is approximately 75-80Â % of the way down the gel.
  13. Turn OFF power, disconnect the electrodes from the power source, and then carefully remove the gel from the gel box.
  14. Using any device that has UV light, visualize your DNA fragments.

Heat shock plasmid transformation (1 -> 2 plasmid)

  1. Set a heat block to 42 Â°C.
  2. Thaw an aliquot chemically competent bacteria on ice.
  3. Add plasmid DNA to the aliquot and mix gently by flicking the tube.
  4. Incubate the bacteria on ice for 10 min to 30 min.
  5. Heat shock the bacteria for exactly 30 s (20 s for E. coli BL21(DE3)).
  6. Incubate the bacteria on ice for approx. 5 min.
  7. Add 1 ml of SOC or LB medium and recover the plasmids for 1 h at 37 Â°C while shaking (optional if the new plasmid only adds an Ampicillin resistance).
  8. Optionally spin down the recovered suspension and resuspend it in a minimal volume of medium.
  9. Plate the suspension on agar supplemented with the appropriate antibiotics (fill in the appropriate protocol here!).

Recovery of plasmid DNA from filter paper

  1. Use clean gloves and cut the marked circle area that contains the dried plasmid DNA.
  2. Using clean forceps, insert the filter paper into a 1.5 ml micro centrifuge tube. Add 50 Âµl of pure water (or until the paper is completely soaked), vortex briefly and incubate at room temperature for 5 minutes. Vortex again and centrifuge the tube for a few seconds.
  3. Remove about 5 Âµl of supernatant for use in transfecting E. coli by electroporation or chemical means. Please do not try to use the DNA directly for any application other than to transform bacteria and prepare a plasmid stock.
  4. Store the remainder of the filter paper/water mix at -20 or -80 Â°C as a permanent archive in case that your plasmid stock ever gets lost or if something turns out to be wrong with it.

Expression of Dnmt1 (linear)

  1. Transform the plasmid harboring DNMT1 (Kanamycin resistant) into Rosetta DE3 competent cells (Chloramphenicol resistant) ( negative control untransformed)
  2. Inoculate a few colonies to 40 ml LB+Kan+Cm medium to grow the cells overnight at 37 Â°C (Prepare 4l LB+Kan+Cm medium to autoclave)
  3. Dispense 10 ml of the growth culture into each 1 l flask and grow the cells at 37 Â°C. When the cell OD reaches ~0.6, lower the temperature to 15-20 Â°C
  4. 30 min later, add IPTG (0.4 mM final concentration) and ZnCl2 (0.1 mM final concentration) into the media and continue to grow the cells overnight.
  5. Spin down the cells at 4000 rpm for 20 min (4 Â°C, 5th or 6th floor, fill 500 ml buckets for Ja10 rotor to 375 ml), and resuspend the cell paste in HisTrap loading buffer, with approximately 20 ml buffer for each liter of cell paste. (Take sample S1, accordingly C1 from untransformed control)
  6. SONICATION of the cells (Lohmann group, cold room 4th floor) while keeping them on ice, disrupt the cells using sonication. To avoid the protein degradation and aggregation, add PMSF and DTT to 1 Î¼M and 0.5 mM respectively.
  7. Place the sonication tip in the sample, close to the bottom, but not touching the tube. Select the right program: Microtip: yes; Amplitude 60; Working time 2 min; On 0.02; Off 0.05 After 5 minutes, check if the sample did not heat up and repeat the program one more time. Clean the tip with some ethanol after use.
  8. After the cell is lysed, spin down the cell lysate using high speed or ultracentrifuge (17000 rpm x 45 min, Ti70 rotor, thick wall tubes [355631], filled to 15 ml)
  9. Collect the supernatant. If any aggregates or particles are visible, filter the supernatant with a 0.2 Âµm filter using a syringe. (Take sample from supernatant S2 and pellet S3, accordingly C2 and C3 from untransformed control)
  10. Load the supernatant to pre-equilibrated HisTrap column, wash the column using 200 ml of chilled His trap loading buffer, and elute the fusion protein using elution buffer.
  11. Collect the eluent and dialyze the protein sample against dialysis buffer overnight at 4C. > Optional: adding protease inhibitor cocktail tablets will help DNMT1 from proteolytic degradation.
  12. Take out sample from dialysis tube, spin down the sample and remove any precipitate
  13. Load the supernatant to the Heparin column, then apply the salt gradient with Buffer A and B. The target protein is expected to elute at 0.4-0.5 M NaCl.
  14. Collect the fractions and run the SDS Page to examine the protein (~100 kD). Pool together the protein fraction with significant purity
  15. Concentrate the fractions to about 1 ml using concentration spin columns (25MWCO)
  16. The protein is finally purified through a 16/60 Superdex 200 size exclusion column using Gel filtration buffer
  17. After the gel filtration, the protein will be examined by SDS page. The fractions with >90% purity will be pooled
  18. Concentration of sample and storage at -80C freezer.

  1. Design of the insert and backbone primers
  • Assemble your desired construct in silico by pasting the fragments. Feel free to add tags and other small stuff between them.
  • Define a âhomology regionâ with a Tm of 60 Â°C - 70 Â°C (~15 - 35 bp) at each fragment border. The Tms of all homology regions should be very similar. It is your choice whether the regions are right next to the border in one fragment, include parts of both fragments, include inserted sequences. These regions should be checked carefully for annoying secondary structures.
  • You need a forward and a reverse primer for any fragment. Any primer consists of a part that binds to the template DNA and an overlap. The binding part should have a G or C at the 3â end and a Tm between 60 Â°C and 72 Â°C. The binding part Tms of one primer pair should be similar. In addition to the binding part, any primer must have an overlap part. Just extend the primers until the chosen homology region is fully covered. Check the complete primers for secondary structures. Lysozyme Assay

Bacteria were transformed with lambda lysozyme constructs, grown to an OD of 0.6 and induced with 1 mM IPTG.

After 4 hours of expression, the samples were diluted to the same OD of 2.0 and centrifuged down at 2,850 rpm. The pellet was resuspended in 10 mM potassium phosphate buffer with a pH of 6.24 and sonicated for two minutes on ice. After centrifugation a second time at 2,850 rpm, the supernatant containing the lambda lysozyme was kept.

For substrate preparation, the lyophilized cells of M.lysodeikticus (Sigma Aldrich), were resuspended in ultrapure water. The supernantant with the protein mix was transfered and aliquoted for biological replicates and to prepare dilution series.

Subsequently the samples were transfered into the thermocycler to do a one minute heat-shock in a temperature span between 45 and 55Â°C. After mixing enzyme and substrate the OD was measured every two minutes in a plate reader over 100 minutes at 37Â°C (figure 4). === Flourescence labeled peptidoglycane assay The expression of the lysozyme samples for the assay using FITC-labeled peptidoglycan was carried out in the same way as we did it in the first assay. We resuspended the pellet in PBS. The protocol of labeling of peptidoglycan with fluorescein isothiocyanate (FITC) you can find in this methods section as well.
