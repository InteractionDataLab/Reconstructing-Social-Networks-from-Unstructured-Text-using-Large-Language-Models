Negligible risks to the general public. Negligible environmental risks, besides improper disposal of chemicals. Negligible risk from mis-use. Risk Reduction: We are maintaining safe lab practices to avoid spills, and using only BSL1 organisms. Potential New Risks: If successfully engineered, the P. acnes bacterium could pose a risk by taking up residence on the skin of individuals working in the lab. An especially viable strain of P. acnes could replace other skin bacteria and begin expressing harmful genes. If the knowledge to transform P. acnes became widely available, then malicious groups could mis-use P. acnes as an infectious agent, spread through skin-skin contact. Potential Risk Reduction: In the future, extra laboratory precautions would be taken to avoid skin exposure when working with engineered P. acnes. A kill switch or genetic fence may also be engineered to contain loose strains of P. acnes. At the University of Pittsburgh, there exists an Institutional Biosafety Committee (IBC), who is responsible for monitoring and ensuring compliance with NIH Guidelines on all activities that involve recombinant DNA (rDNA) including gene therapy. We have not discussed our project with them.
