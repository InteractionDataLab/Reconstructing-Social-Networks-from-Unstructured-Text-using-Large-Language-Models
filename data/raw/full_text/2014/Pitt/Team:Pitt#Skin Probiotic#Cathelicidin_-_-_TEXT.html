Using the transformation protocol optimized by our team, we can insert novel genes for beneficent functions into P. acnes. One such function is the secretion of an anti-microbial peptide, which would reduce inflammation by inhibiting the growth of bacteria in the area. Cathelicidin is a natural antibiotic produced by the immune cells of many mammals, including humans. The antimicrobial peptide gene will be constructed into the plasmid along with an inducible blue-light promoter. If successful, the bacteria containing the plasmid will produce the cathelicidin when exposed to blue light and in turn suppress the P. acnes population when it becomes elevated.
