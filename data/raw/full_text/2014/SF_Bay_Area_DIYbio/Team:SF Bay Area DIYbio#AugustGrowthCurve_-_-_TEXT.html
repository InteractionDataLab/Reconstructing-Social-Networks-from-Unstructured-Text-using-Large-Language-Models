Also, this data will elucidate the effects of heterologous protein expression on our strains. Here's a brief protocol for monitoring yeast growth. GROWTH CURVE Streak Carolina strain on a YPD + Ura plate for isolated colonies. Grow overnight at 30C Pick a single colony from the plate (in triplicate) and grow in 5mL YPD + Ura The next day, Record the OD (650nm) of the >16hour culture, and dilute the Growth Curve Culture by adding 100uL of 16hour culture to 99.9mL of YPD + Ura. Grow at 30C, 250rpm. Record the OD (650nm) at 0, 5, 10, 24, 48, and 72 hours. Use YPD + Ura as the blank for each reading. Repeat growth curve with pD1214:FAKS-hKappa cloned into yeast strain.'' no, it's not the real human kappa casein, but it'll get us closer to understanding how our strains will perform after real cloning happens. This is a great set of experiments for folks who've not done cloning before. Please learn about Bacterial transformation and plating for single colonies, Plasmid Prep, and Yeast transformation. Transform positive plasmid into E. coli (see Molecular Biology page) Instead of plating transformation, add the transformation reaction to 5mL of LB + Carb(50). Grow this plasmid overnight at 37C, rotating; and plasmid prep the DNA the next day. Transform yeast using the Yeast Transformation protocol. Plate transformation and incubate overnight at 30C. Select single colonies and repeat from (1) - (c) (above). This experiment can be repeated with different starting concentrations of Yeast culture (1:2, 1:10, 1:50, 1:100, 1:500, 1:1000).
