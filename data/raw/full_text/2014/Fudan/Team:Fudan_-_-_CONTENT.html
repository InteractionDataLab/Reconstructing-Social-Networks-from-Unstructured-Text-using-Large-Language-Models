Shanghai

Timeline

2014 October

We were debugging our previous design, and we were making our wiki pages and poster. Giant jamboree was waiting for us!

2014 September

We successfully directly converted the MEF to iPSc with our design. Visas and air tickets were prepared. We still needed to work out the other part of our project for the limit time.

2014 August

Our iGEM lab was still not available, but luckily our instructors supplied us with their labs. We could start our project again. We all felt excited! Besides, we attended the meets up held by NCTU Formosa, we all learnt a lot in that meets up.

2014 July

We encountered great difficulties and problems -- The school of life sciences, where we were doing experiments before, made a move to a new place (Our new school region). During this chaotic time, our lab had been strongly destroyed into a mass. Many plasmids and DNA we made successfully before got lost, and we all felt very upset.

2014 June

We needed to take the final examinations , so we had a quite long rest for iGEM.

2014 May

We helped Tongji iGEM team with their project and wikis, and at the same time we started the wet experiments of the project. We constructed the first plasmid we need successfully!

2014 April

Our lab was constructed successfully, and our design of project was completed preliminarily. So we started the training for our wet team members.

2014 March

We formed our team. For we did not have any iGEM lab before (previous works were all be done in a professor’s lab, but this year it could not be permitted any more.), we began to build our own iGEM lab, and at the same time, we invited our advisors and instructors to join us. In late March, the brain storm was coming -- We started our design!

2014 February

We learnt of iGEM competition from the previous team members of Fudan iGEM team. We started to do some self-learning about synthetic biology, and we also held some salons for synthetic biology.
