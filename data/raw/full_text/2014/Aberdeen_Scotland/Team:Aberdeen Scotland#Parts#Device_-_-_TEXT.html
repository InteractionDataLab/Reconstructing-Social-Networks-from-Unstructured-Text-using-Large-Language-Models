Thus it was built from bits and pieces in order to do a preliminary test and verify its viability. The preliminary data from the tests showed that the detector was able to differentiate between GFP producing and non-producing bacteria. Fig.1 Series dilutions to estimate detector sensitivity From Fig.1 we can see that the detector can easily distinguish the GFP producing culture from below 1/10 dilutions. Thus we continued on and improved the design by soldering some of the circuit and making it more compact. Then we made more test with the Sender and Receiver bacteria. Fig.2 Detector response to Sender and Receiver cultures Again the detector is sensitive enough to pick up the GFP in the Receiver. For the final test, we made clean overnight cultures of the Receiver and Sender and mixed them in a growing culture. This was done to see after how long the detector is going to pick up the GFP production. Hence, if our diagnostic method is going to work. Fig.3 Detector response to QS GFP-producing Receiver and Sender culture, normalised against LB medium Finally, the simple detector proved capable of detecting the QS culture, thus confirming our design success.
