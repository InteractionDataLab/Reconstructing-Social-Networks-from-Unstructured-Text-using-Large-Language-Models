Microbial fuel cells (MFCs) generate electricity via the anaerobic metabolism of certain types of bacteria. Such electrogenic organisms are able to transfer electrons during the metabolism of organic material. Naturally, it follows that, with a little bit of design, these bacteria can be used to generate a current in a fuel cell. There is definite possibility that MFCs could be used extensively as a means to collect energy from waste water. Therefore, we have set out to create our own MFC, in a project where life and electronics aggregate together. On Our Bacteria of Choice While there are several known electrogenic bacteria known to exist, we have chosen Shewanella oneidensis MR-1 .
