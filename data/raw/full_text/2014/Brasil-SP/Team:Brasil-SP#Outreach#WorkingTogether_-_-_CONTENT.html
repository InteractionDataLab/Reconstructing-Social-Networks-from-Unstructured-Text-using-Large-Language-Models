    • Skype Chats!

         Despite the fact the conversation happened between Sao Carlos (Brasil) and Oslo (Norway) this conversation was spoken in portuguese! Izadora is a brazilian student living in Oslo. We exchanged experiences about the struggle to assemble a team, and what are the different difficulties we had during the project development. It was really interesting clashing this contrasting experiences of living an iGEM competition.

    The Visit!

         On April 24, 2014, our team was visited by Dr. Carlos Gustavo Nunes Silva, Instructor of the Federal University of Amazonas Team (UFAM_Brazil). He presented his experience in previous editions of the iGEM competition, ongoing projects, and gave us important advices. Dr. Nunes Silva also spoke about the philosophy of the competition and teamwork, and how iGEM initiative enhances the training of young researchers. This networking was crucial -- he questioned us about our skills and lab infrastructure, and thus, we realized we had real conditions to participate in iGEM 2014. Here we are!

    iGEM Teams collaborating with our survey!

         Our survey was also applied to virtually all iGEM Teams and other Universities worldwide, with representation from every continent. To date, we have received 64 responses out of Brazil, from all five continents, as follows:

    Africa (Egypt), America (USA, Canada, Mexico), Asia (Israel, ​Japan), Europe (Belgium, France, Germany, Greece, Italy, Netherlands​, Poland, Spain, United Kingdom), Oceania (Australia, New Zealand).

         Interestingly, 22 respondents are freshmen or sophomores.

         Unfortunately the number of responses coming from abroad is still low, small, and this prevents us from performing a statistical analysis with consistency and validity. To have a more precise analysis, we are planing to apply our survey in a larger sample, so that we can compare these result against data collected in Brazil.
