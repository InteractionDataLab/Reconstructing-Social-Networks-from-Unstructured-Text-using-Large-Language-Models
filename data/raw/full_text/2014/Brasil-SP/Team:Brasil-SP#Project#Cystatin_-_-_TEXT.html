It is an excellent biomarker for renal dysfunction, specially for Chronic Kidney Disease (CDK), due to its constant rate in the blood and its independence of the aforementioned variables (diet, gender, ethnicity, age, muscle mass, and others), varying only with changes in the glomerular filtration rate (GFR). More specifically, Cys C levels rises when GFR descreases and falls when the GFR increases (ABRAHAMSON, 2014; SARKAR et al, 2005; WASUNG et al, 2014). In order to work with a system capable of detecting a inhibitor like Cys C, we looked for an enzyme that has its activity inhibited specifically by the Cys C so that we could measure the Cys C levels through alteration in the enzyme activity.
