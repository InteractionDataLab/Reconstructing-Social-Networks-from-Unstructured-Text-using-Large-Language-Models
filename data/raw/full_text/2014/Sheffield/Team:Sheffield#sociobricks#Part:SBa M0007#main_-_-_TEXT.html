Instead of testing a hypothesis as seen in other research methods, there is a strong emphasis on exploring the nature of social phenomenon. A typical ethnography contains a brief history and analysis of the environment or habitat which makes a substantial contribution towards the understand of the social life of humans as it is a credible reality. Essentially, all observed behaviour is recorded and symbol-meaning relations are described using concepts to avoid casual explanations.
