PCR instructions: Touch with a pipette tip the colony on the plate and try to get as much bacteria as possible Touch now the bottom of a PCR reaction tube and turn the tip several times Take this tip and make a small streak out on a plate (LB + antibiotics) and incubate the plate at 37°C during a day or overnight Mix primers and water When everything is ready, take the MM out of the fridge, add it to the primers and the water and bring the MM as soon as possible back into the fridge Mix the PCR mix and give 20 µl in each PCR reaction tube (either using a new tip or ensure that you don’t get in contact with any DNA) Close the tubes properly, mix them gently with the vortexer and spin them shortly down Start the PCR PCR programme: Initial denaturation 95°C 5 min (if it’s not a colony PCR, take only 2 min) Denaturation 95°C 30 s Annealing 55°C* 30s Extension 68°C 1 min/1 kb fragment length Final extension 68°C 2 min * or the appropriate temperature for the primers Retrieved from " http://2008.igem.org/Team:BCCS-Bristol/Protocols-Colony_PCR " Recent changes What links here Related changes Special pages My preferences Printable version Permanent link Privacy policy Disclaimers
