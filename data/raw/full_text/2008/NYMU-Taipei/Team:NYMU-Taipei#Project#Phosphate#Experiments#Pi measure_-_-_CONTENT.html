    7/30, 2008 PM 8:00

    • 500, 1000, 1500, 2000, 2500, 3000 uM pi
    • reaction time: 11 mins (the timing of measure is also important)
    • 5N H2SO4
    • Because the high [pi] is out of the linear range, we have to dilute the samples into linear region
    • standard samples are diluted from 10,000 uM stock
    • The dilution procedure are:
    • extract 30 uL from 10,000 uM stock + 70 uL ddH2O, and add 900 uL ddH2O to 1 C.C. (3000uM, 10X dilution)
    • extract 25 uL from 10,000 uM stock + 75 uL ddH2O, and add 900 uL ddH2O to 1 C.C. (2500uM, 10X dilution)
    • extract 20 uL from 10,000 uM stock + 80 uL ddH2O, and add 900 uL ddH2O to 1 C.C. (2000uM, 10X dilution)
    • extract 15 uL from 10,000 uM stock + 85 uL ddH2O, and add 900 uL ddH2O to 1 C.C. (1500uM, 10X dilution)
    • extract 10 uL from 10,000 uM stock + 90 uL ddH2O, and add 900 uL ddH2O to 1 C.C. (1000uM, 10X dilution)
    • extract 05 uL from 10,000 uM stock + 95 uL ddH2O, and add 900 uL ddH2O to 1 C.C. (0500uM, 10X dilution)
    • However, the precision of this dilution procedure is based on the how precise u can extract from 10,000 uM stock
    • Results
    • 1.8472 (3000 uM, 10X dilution)
    • 1.5183 (2500 uM, 10X dilution)
    • 1.8385 (2000 uM, 10X dilution)
    • 1.7027 (1500 uM, 10X dilution)
    • 1.8300 (1000 uM, 10X dilution)
    • 1.7248 (0500 uM, 10X dilution)
    • overall R2 is -0.0792

    8/14, 2008 Trial #3 Remark

    • all trials show when phosphate above 300 uM to 1000 uM, the measures are not linear as expectation
    • the reaction time 10 mins may not be the best choice when we don't know anything about the kinetics
    • two instruments (Thermo Scientific Varioskan Flash and BECKMAN DU-640) do not have significant measuring difference
