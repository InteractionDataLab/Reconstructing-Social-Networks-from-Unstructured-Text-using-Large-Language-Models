Prepare agar plate with the correct antibiotic. Draw a grid with as many squares as the colonies you’re screening. 2. Label PCR tubes for each colony and one control (no DNA or colony) 3. Add 6µL of PCR H2O MQ to each PCR tube 4. Pick a colony from the agar plate with a pipette tip and resuspend in 6 µL PCR H2O. (if inoculating LB, resuspend in 7 µL PCR H2O) 5. Add 1 µL of resuspended DNA to a grid square. Incubate overnight at 37C. 6. Optional: Add 1 µL of resuspended DNA in corresponding LB tube and inoculate overnight 7. Prepare master mix. Vortex master mix before adding Taq. Once you added Taq mix gently. 8. Add 20 µL of Master Mix to each PCR tube. 9. Centrifuge briefly in the PCR machine. Use the pulse option. 10. Start the PCR machine on the desired cycle.
