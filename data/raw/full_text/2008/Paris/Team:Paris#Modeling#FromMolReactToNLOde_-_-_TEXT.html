The idea of the Characterization Approach is that this modelization must both accounts for every small steps of the system and allow the experimental characterizations . Therefore, the following equations do not describe properly what really happens in the cells. For exemple, we know that the transcription factor FlhD-FlhC is actually an hexamere FlhD4C2. But, as we will surely not get access to the dissociation constant of the hexamerisation, we just treat it as an abstract inducer protein "FlhDC", with an order (n) in its complexation caracterization probably around 2*4 = 8 (but perhaps completly different ! ; the estimation of the error by the Parameters Finder Programs would tell us if it stays consistent). ↓ all assumptions ↑ Polymere Transcription TF Competition Degradation/Dilution RBS issue Mathematical Interpretation and Simulation of the Molecular Reactions First, we consider the complexation phenomenon.
