    • MOSFETMonitoring and Operating System Founded on Engineered T cells
    One of the main design goals in engineering is to improve SYSTEM STABILITY by reducing noise. Similarly, in designing synthetically engineered genetic circuits, we try to reduce the impact of complex intracellular environment on gene circuits, especially in mammalian cells.
    Therefore, we present our design to solve the problem of NOISE in mammalian cells and wider applications of synthetic biology on them hoping to make further contribution to HUMAN HEALTH.
    We, the iGEM team of NAU, inspired by MOSFETs, simulate the function of a MOSFET with designed genetic circuits in mammalian cells.
    On top of that, we also carry out simulations with MODELSof the gene circuits.
    For more iGEMers and teams' communication, our team develop an APP called iGEMCloud.
