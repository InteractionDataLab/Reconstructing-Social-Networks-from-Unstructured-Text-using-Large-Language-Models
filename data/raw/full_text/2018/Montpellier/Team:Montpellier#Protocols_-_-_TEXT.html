Glycerol allows to reduce the harmful effect of ice crystals of bacteria which can damage cells by dehydration caused by a localized increase in salt concentration leading to denaturation of proteins. Additionally, ice crystals can also puncture cellular membranes. Glycerol as a cryoprotectant depresses the freezing point of bacterial cells, enhancing supercooling. It does so by forming strong hydrogen bonds with water molecules, competing with water-water hydrogen bonding. This disrupts the crystal lattice formation of ice unless the temperature is significantly lowered. The final concentration have to be no more than 20%. For even longer storage, freeze DNA at -80Â°C Cleaning procedures Cleaning procedure for beads Used beads should be placed in recipient with bleach before washing. Put the beads (without the bleach) in a bucket. Add new bleach and water to cover all of the beads, wait 30min for full decontamination. Remove the bleach, place the beads in a clean bucket and add water. Mix beads and water to be sure that all beads have been in contact with the water. Re-iterate the previous step. Perform twice step 3 with deionized water instead of water. Perform once step 3 with ethanol 70%. Remove ethanol. Clean your bench. Dry beads using paper (place paper on your bench, smoothly put beads on it and use paper to dry them). Place beads into small bottles (you can use the paper as a funnel). Autoclave the bottles (you can just add autoclave tap and place them on kitchen table). Cleaning procedure for electroporation cuvettes Materials : Dirty Gene Pulser / MicroPulser Electroporation cuvettes 2 500mL beakers 1L of 70% ethanol Protocol : Arrange the cuvettes side by side in two beakers identified A and B. Fill cuvettes with 70% ethanol. Add other lying cuvettes and remove the caps on top. Fill the beakers up to 500mL with ethanol. Warning : all cuvettes must be filled. Cover the beakers of double aluminum foil. Sonicate the beakers alternatively 15min A, 15min B, 15min A, 15min B, in the sonication bath. Cool on the bench overnight. Empty the ethanol from the beaker by opening minimally the aluminum foil (to avoid contamination). Make 5 to 8 holes in the aluminum of each beakers with a syringe needle to create a ventilation. Dry the cuvettes in their beakers in an oven at 65Â°C (2 days or more). When the tanks and caps are dry, close them under sterile condition (flame or under the hood). Cleaning procedure for 96 wells plates Clean recommandation : 96 wells plate should be clean just after usage (at least in the 48h). Afterward cells tend to precipitate and it starts to be trickier to well clean them. If 96 well plates are store for a long time before cleaning, it is important to carefully control the plate after cleaning. Do not hesitate to throw plate which are not perfectly clean (and white). Protocol : Throw the liquid culture from the plate in a bucket and add bleach to it. After 30min, throw the liquid to the sink and clean the bucket and the sink. Place the plate to a bucket and add bleach mix to water to completely fill the plate. Wait 30min for complete decontamination of the plate. (do not wait several days, it obstructs the sink and the plate will turn yellow with time). Remove the bleach. Clean the plate with water, repeat this step twice. You can use the tap to add water specifically to each well. Remove as much as possible liquid from the plate between two rounds of cleaning by manually shaking the plate. Clean the plate with deionized water, repeat this step twice. Clean the plate with ethanol, repeat this step twice. Remove liquid from the plate and let it dry on paper in the reverse orientation during at least one day (depending the external temperature and humidity percentage). Control the quality of the cleaning, if the plate is not perfectly clean: throw it, if it is clean, place it on the shelf with it friends.
