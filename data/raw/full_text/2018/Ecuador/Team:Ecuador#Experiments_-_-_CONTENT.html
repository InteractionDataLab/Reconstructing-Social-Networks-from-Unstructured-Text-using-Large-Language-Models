PHASE I: CONSTRUCTION OF THE COMPOSITE PARTS CBD cipA-BMP2 and CBD cipA-sfGFP
Experiment 1: Competent cells preparation
E.coli cells DH5α, BL21 (DE3) and BL21 (DE3) pLyss were prepared using the CaCl2 method, slightly modifying the one described in:
Competent Cells
PROTOCOL
  1. Autoclave the glass and plastic material (Petri dishes, conical centrifuge tubes, Erlenmeyers) filled ¾ with distilled water to make them free of detergent since any residue is an inhibitor of the growth and transformation of competent cells.
  2. Inoculate 125 mL of LB medium with 1 mL of E. coli cell stock and culture overnight at room temperature. A spectrophotometer or Nanodrop can be used to determine an OD 600nm ≤ 0.3.
  3. Pre-freeze the conical centrifuge tubes.
  4. Transfer the culture to the prechilled conical centrifuge tubes.
  5. Centrifuge for 10 minutes at 3000 g at 4 ° C.
  6. Gently resuspend by vortexing the cell pellet in 40 mL of ice cold CCMB80 buffer.
  7. Hold on ice for 20 minutes.
  8. Centrifuge again for 10 minutes at 3000 g at 4 ° C.
  9. Resuspend by pipetting the cell pellet in 15 mL of ice cold CCMB80 buffer.
  10. Hold on ice for 20 minutes.
  11. Aliquot in cryotubes 2 mL.
  12. Store at -80 ° C.

Experiment 5: Transformation
The psb1c3-CBD vectors cipA-BMP2 and psb1c3-CBD cipA-sfGFP obtained by fusion assembly were transformed into E. coli DH5α, BL21 (DE3) cells by heath shock method according to the protocol described in Transformation.
PROTOCOL
  1. Add 70μl of competent cells in a 2ml Eppendorf tube.
  2. Add 8 μl of resuspended DNA in the tube.
    3. Gently pipet up and down several times. Keep all the tubes on ice.
  3. Close the 2 ml tubes and incubate on ice for 20 minutes
  4. For thermal shock place the tubes in the water bath at 42 ° C for 45 seconds
    5. The 2 ml tubes should be in a rack for floating foam tubes. Place in a water bath to ensure that the bottom of the tubes is submerged. Time is critical
  5. Incubate on ice for 2-4 minutes
  6. Add 800μl of SOC medium in each transformation (Table 1)

Phase IV: Characterization of the binding strength of the CBD cipA with an endogenous C-terminal linker
Measurements of remaining fluorescences, after different washes, were done according to the protocol described by the Team Imperial 2014. Briefly, 20g of kombucha cellulose were blended with 125 mL. Then, 100 uL of the mixture were added into each well of a 96-plate reader and dried at 60º for 5.5 hours using a stove. A volume of 100 uL of CBD cipA-sfGFP protein extract was added into each well an incubated at 4ºC overnight. The fluorescence was measured after washing with different substances: dH20, 70% EtOH, Plasma and Stif.
