    Kevin Fitzgerald

    Major: Biological Engineering

    Year: Junior

    Kevin Fitzgerald is an aspiring Calvin Klein model, a Junior in Biological Engineering, and most importantly, a Gemini. He grew up on the mines of suburban Cincinnati, OH but currently lives in Boston, MA. If modelling falls through (it really is a volatile job market), Kevin plans on either working at a small biotechnology company or furthering his education in synthetic biology. His hobby includes screaming when digests don’t work, coinciding with a periodic questioning of the existence of a higher power.

    Makayla Schacht

    Major: Genetics

    Year: Junior

    Makayla Schacht is a Junior this year at Purdue University studying genetics. Her initial dream was to travel the world pursuing research in synthetic biology, but her mind constantly changes. Nevertheless, she plans on pursuing a master’s degree after her undergraduate degree at Purdue. When she isn’t trying to pipet infinitesimal amounts of various liquids into microcentrifuge tubes, she is probably sleeping, worrying about everything else, procrastinating because she doesn't know where to start, or practicing music. Outside of academics, she is a trombonist in the Purdue All-American Marching Band with a wicked glove tan line. She has performed at football games and internationally at the St. Patrick’s Day Parade in Dublin, Ireland.

    Janice Chan

    Janice Chan is a Junior, all the way from Phoenix, Arizona. She loves watching T.V, eating ice cream, meeting new people and traveling to new places.     Zachary Berglund

    Major: Biological Engineering

    Minor: Biotechnology & Biology

    Year: Junior

When he’s done with his education, he ultimately wants to end up as a professor since his ultimate goal is to have a busy and fufilling life.

    Julianne Dejoie

    Major: Genetics & Spanish

    Minor: Biotechnology

    Year: Junior

    Julianne Dejoie is a Junior from Lafayette, IN. Besides for her love of synthetic biology and genetics, Julianne also loves robotics and manufacturing.     Zach Hartley

    Major: Plant Genetics and Biotechnology

    Minor: Biological Sciences

    Year: Sophomore

    To the dismay of all of his friends and family, Zach Hartley left sunny California to enter the chaotic, flat lands of Indiana. After Purdue, he plans on pursuing graduate school or a career at a biotechnology company.     Moiz Rasheed

    Caleigh Roleck

    Major: Biochemistry

    Minor: Biotechnology & Statistics

    Year: Senior

    Caleigh Roleck, the token senior in Purdue iGEM, is the girl Purdue iGEM cannot get rid of, not matter how hard they try, but let’s face it, they would not want to anyways. She is majoring in biochemistry and minoring in biotechnology and statistics, and gives Zach Berglund a run for his money in terms of energy drinks (specifically Red Bull) consumed in the average day. In addition to running the new member program for the iGEM recruitees, she serves as the president of Purdue’s Students for Responsible Science and Engineering, which plans campus-wide events focused on the integration of science into society, and serves as a teaching assistant for the introductory biology laboratory course. She works in a research laboratory studying how two different bacteria are able to genetically transform plants. Her long-term career goals is to earn her doctorate and serve as a federal researcher, and her long-term personal goal is to be her neighborhood’s crazy bird lady.

    Jamie Arabshahi

    Sarah Liu

    Major: Biological Engineering

    Minor: Forensic Sciences

    Year: Junior

    Sarah Liu is a junior in Biological Engineering minoring in Forensic Sciences from Carmel, IN, but her family residence post high school graduation has moved from there to Houston, TX and then again to Philadelphia, PA in the past two years. She won’t be offended if you trash talk her hometown since she acknowledges its status as most hated city in Indiana. She’s the peasant third wheel to the team as a graphic designer and photographer, but she IS the one who designed the team’s banner that you will see at the Jamboree and shot several of the team’s photos. She can’t get away with not practicing violin as current concertmaster of the Purdue Philharmonic Orchestra and member of the pit orchestra of Purdue Theatre’s upcoming production of Next to Normal (Nov. 9-18, 2018 - support Sarah and buy tickets at https://tinyurl.com/buyPurdueN2Ntixhere), and she does research in the Y-lab (yangyanglab.org). In the little free time she has left, she either sleeps (or doesn’t), procrastinates, dabbles in making art, shoots photos for the Purdue Exponent in which her mastery of the art can capture a non-blurry still shot of Zachary Berglund in motion, enjoys traveling as a proctor/volunteer in Science Olympiad tournaments everywhere, and exercises power to ban misbehaving people (or spambots) online as a Global Moderator of scioly.org. She eventually hopes to pursue a PhD and a career in biotechnology R&D, particularly in medicine although she’s pretty flexible about that, but if that falls through, being a real CSI unlike the ones on TV is a decent option to her too.
