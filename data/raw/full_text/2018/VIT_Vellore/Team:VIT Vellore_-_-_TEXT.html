LexA then binds to its operator region in the initial plasmid, and represses carbonic anhydrase LexA functions as a repressor in the above figure. However, as the pH starts to become acidic again, recA protease is produced recA protease binds to LexA (repressor) and lyses it, thereby preventing its action. Carbonic anhydrase is produced again, and pH homeostasis is ensured
