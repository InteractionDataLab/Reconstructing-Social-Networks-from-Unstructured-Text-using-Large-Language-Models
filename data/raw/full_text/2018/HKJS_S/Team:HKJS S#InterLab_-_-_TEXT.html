Laboratories from around the world conducted these experiments. In this manner, their studies are trustworthy and repeatable, the key component of scientific discipline. Thus, other related labs will be able to build upon others’ work. Indeed, fluorescence is a property commonly measured in synthetic biology, but data from different labs are not comparable due to different units have been used. The data has been processed differently and the outcome is given in ‘relative expression’. The InterLab study aims to counter this issue by using detailed protocol and data analysis to yield absolute units for measuring GFP. This year, we are focusing on reducing variable fluorescence measurements between different labs by normalizing to absolute cell count or colony-forming units (CFUs) instead of OD.
