At high levels of AraC, it self regulates, reducing its own transcription rate. 7 figure 1 8 AraO2 In the absence of arabinose, one of the monomers of AraC protein binds to the AraO2 while the other binds to the half-site araI1. This then facilitates the formation of a DNA loop as the dimerization of two AraC is energetically favorable. 1 figure 2 9 AraO1 When there is an excess of AraC, there is a greater likelihood for them to bind at AraO1. When AraC is bound to AraO1, it sterically prevents the RNA polymerase from binding to the AraC promoter. 2 CAP In the absence of glucose, there is a high level of cyclic AMP/ CAP, thus making it more likely to bind to the CAP binding site. Binding of CAP in the binding site then prevents the formation of DNA loops between araO1 and araI1. This increases the likelihood of AraC binding to araI2 which facilitates the binding of RNA polymerase to the promoter of our gene of interest. 3 AraC Promoter The promoter that allows for the transcription of our gene of interest.
