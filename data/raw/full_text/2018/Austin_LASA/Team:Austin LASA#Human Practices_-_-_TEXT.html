When conversing with the NGO official on HIV detection kits, a recurring point that was brought up, was how is this kit different from the market and what is the cost. In this manner, the cost is especially important because our kit was developed to target those in the rural location who have a higher risk of HIV. Thereby, in order for this kit to be able to reach its target audience, the cost must be competitive with other existing models. Creating a kit with an idealistic purpose, but in reality, using the kit for those that can afford it undermine both the purpose and the ethics of such a project. In an effort to keep costs low, we utilized methods to efficiently organize the materials such as aliquoting to prevent contamination and only provide the necessary quantities. Similarly, the protocols for our kit do not require expensive machinery like other tests. This helps to not only keep the overall price low but allows us to use the kit in laboratories without much equipment.
