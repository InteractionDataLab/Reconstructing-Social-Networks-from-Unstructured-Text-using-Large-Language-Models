Take out the 5 mL overnight culture solution, add it to the centrifuge tube, then centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 1 min, and try to aspirate the supernatant. Then collect the precipitate into a centrifuge tube. 3. Add 250 μL P1 to the centrifuge tube with the cell pellet (RNaseA has been added). Make sure the bacterial pellet is thoroughly suspended using a pipette or a vortex shaker. 4. Add 250 μL P2 to the centrifuge tube and gently invert it 6-8 times to fully lyse the cells. Mix them gently, don't violently oscillate, so as not to interrupt the genomic DNA, causing the extracted plasmid to be mixed with genomic DNA fragments. At this point, the bacterial solution should become clear and viscous, but the time should not exceed 5 min to avoid damaging the plasmid. 5. Add 350 μL P3 to the centrifuge tube, gently flip it up and down for 6-8 times, mix them well, and a white flocculent precipitate will appear. Centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 10 min. 6. Transfer the supernatant collected in the previous step to the adsorption column CP3 (the adsorption column is placed in the collection tube), be sure not to aspirate the precipitation. Centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 30-60 sec, drain the waste from the collection tube, and place the adsorption column CP3 into the collection tube.
