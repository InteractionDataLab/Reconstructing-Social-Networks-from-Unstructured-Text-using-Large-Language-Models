    • Overview

    When we started

    The genome of organisms contains commands for basic biological functions like assimilation, reproduction, communication, motility and dissimilation. Meanwhile, cells can also be reprogrammed by exogenous DNA with new genetic information for artificial behaviours and functions. As we know, synthetic biology developed very fast in recent years. One of the most important mission that helps us to build our fantastic dream in synthetic biology is to develop standardized gene regulatory parts. By doing this, the function of artificial circuits will become more predictable and operable. More and more toolbox of modular genetic parts were designed to regulate cellular processes. The gene regulatory toolbox for controlling and remodelling genetic circuits is essential and valuable.

    The existing strategies

    To satisfy various requirements, scientists have already created a lot of gene regulation tools based on three levels: DNA, RNA and protein. Among above three levels, post-transcriptional regulation plays a critical role for gene expression control. Post-transcriptional regulation generally refers to the control on RNA molecules. There are several advantages of post-transcriptional regulation comparing with traditional genetic regulation methods on DNA, like faster response time, simpler regulatory mechanism and diverse design strategies. More and more methods have been created based on post-transcriptional regulation because of its flexibility and practicability [1][2][3]. A typical case is riboswitch which can bind to a small molecule and result in a secondary structure change of mRNA with translation rate tuning of corresponding protein. It is not only a potential biosensor design platform, but also a powerful tool to control the expression level of downstream genes. RNA switch responsive to small RNA is another type. Small RNA could also be engineered as an activator, which is able to regulate the cis-repressive RNA (crRNA) element that masks the ribosome binding site (RBS) of the transcript of particular gene through base pairing [4]. A well-known case named toehold switch has been applied on synthetic biology. Recently, a CRISPR-Csy4 mediated activation method based on cis-repressive RNA cleavage has been achieved in bacteria [5]. This system significantly improved the feasibility of post-transcriptional regulation.

    OUR INNOVATION AND ADVANTAGES

    By reviewing existing methods and strategies, we are aware of some aspects worth optimizing. For general small RNA-mediated riboswitch, if the crRNA is perfectly paired with RBS, the trans-activating RNA will be hard to compete for the binding with crRNA. This may cause low activation efficiency for opening this type of switch. As for toehold switch, each switch needs to be individually designed. At the same time, introducing long homologous sequence for regulation may increase undesired recombination risks of foreign fragment insertion. [6]. This year, we designed and achieved a new alternative regulatory tool named "MiniToe Family", which is a new toolkit based on Csy4 produced by OUC-China.
