    • Cyano Q&A

    A lot of current iGEM teams work with cyanobacteria. These tiny green microorganisms require special cultivation methods which aren’t always common knowledge to everyone. We created this Cyano Q&A especially for iGEM Hamburg 2018 in order to solve the most prominent questions they had and also for other teams entering the world of cyanobacteria.

    • What are the culture conditions of cyanobacteria? It depends on the strain. Typically Synechococcus elongatus sp 7942 and Synechocystis PCC 6803 - two model organisms - are incubated at 30°C (86°F). Moreover, as an autotrophic organism they need light in order to survive. We use light bulbs with a light intensity of 120 μE/m2·s . With those settings they have a growth rate of 0.052 h‐1. Optionally, you can use the light intensity of 60 μE/m2·s. With those adjustment the growth rate would be 0.026 h‐1.
