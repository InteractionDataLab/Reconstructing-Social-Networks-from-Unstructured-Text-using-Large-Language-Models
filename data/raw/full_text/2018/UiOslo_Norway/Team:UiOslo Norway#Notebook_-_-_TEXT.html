Production of chemically competent DH5α cells We performed the production of chemically competent DH5α cells according to this protocol . Since the procedure didn’t work we suspected the cell stock we were using was not optimal. We therefore chose to buy new stock of already chemically competent DH5α cells from Thermofisher. Week 26 Transformation of Interlab test devices We did a transformation of the Interlab test devices 1-6 plus the positive and negative control with the bought chemically competent DH5α cells according to the transformation protocol. After incubation at 37oC overnight we could not observe any colonies. No growth was observed, but the experiment was retried, and we got colonies for each test device and the positive and negative controls. Presumably human error or other factors may have been the cause of this happening. We decided to contact the iGEM headquarter to see if anyone else had reported problems with the Interlab study, but we discovered that we had used the wrong wells. After acknowledging our errors, we were able to start fresh with the correct wells. Always double-check your wells! Week 27 Transformation of Interlab test devices We did a transformation of the Interlab test devices 1-6 (with the correct wells) plus the positive and negative control with the bought chemically competent DH5α cells according to the transformation protocol. After incubation at 37oC, colonies was observed in all plates.
