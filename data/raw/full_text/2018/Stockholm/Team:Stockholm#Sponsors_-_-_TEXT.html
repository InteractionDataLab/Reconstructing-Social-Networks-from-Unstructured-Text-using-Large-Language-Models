Team:Stockholm/Sponsors iGEM Stockholm 2018 Wiki Sponsors Our work would not have been possible if not for the helpful and generous contributions of our sponsors. We reached out to our academic institutions for support as well as established companies in the Biotech and Pharma fields. Karolinska Institutet (KI), founded in 1810, is one of the world's leading and most prestigious medical universities in the world. Around 6,000 students take educational courses on the two campuses in Stockholm. Since 1901, the Nobel Assembly at KI awards the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine [1]. KTH Royal Institute of Technology, founded in 1827, is Sweden’s largest technical research and learning institution. KTH has grown to become one of Europe’s leading technical and engineering universities. The four campuses around Stockholm, gather more than 14,000 students [2]. KI Innovations AB is an incubator that evaluates and verifies promising projects and encourages business growth by offering education, professional networks, financing and expertise [3]. Janssen is a pharmaceutical company of Johnson and Johnson family with more than 30,000 employees committed to prevent some of the most difficult diseases. Janssen is currently developing treatments for patients in important therapeutic areas of healthcare [4]. Johnson & Johnson is the world’s largest and most broadly based healthcare company with more than 130 years of history. Their products greatly influence the trajectory of healthcare [5]. SnapGene offers cost-efficient and easy software to meet the needs of molecular biologists and enables natural planning of their cloning procedures [6]. Merck was founded in Germany in 1668. Its long history and high investment in research and development contributed to its leading position in healthcare, life sciences and performance materials [7]. Promega, with a portfolio of more than 4,000 products, is a global leader in innovative solutions and technical support to scientists [8]. Microsynth is a leading European company in the area of nucleic acid synthesis, sequencing and analysis [9]. Eppendorf is a biotechnology company that develops and sells instruments, consumables, and services in laboratories worldwide [10]. Integrated DNA Technologies (IDT) is widely recognized as the industry leader in the manufacture of custom oligonucleotides for molecular biology applications. It offers innovative tools and solutions for genomics applications. [11] New England Biolabs is a recognized world leader company in the discovery and production of enzymes for molecular biology applications. [12] References Karolinska Institutet (KI).
