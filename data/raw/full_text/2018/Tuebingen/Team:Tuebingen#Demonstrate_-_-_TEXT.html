This would interfere with the process of cell penetration. Cell Culture Botox is a neurotoxin and can therefore only affect cells with a neuronal character. This is also the reason why our project represents such a groundbreaking improvement in application. In order to test our detoxified Botox constructs, we needed cells that were as similar as possible to neurons. Nowadays there are countless different target lines available that are more or less similar. Our chosen target line SHSY5Y had the advantage that there was already a detailed differentiation protocol available . In the beginning, the neuroblastoma were maintained for some time to get a feel for their behavior. For the final differentiation a fresh vial SHSY5Y Passage 14 was thawed and grown in a culture bottle with Basic Culture Media for one week. The culture was split and divided into 6-well plates with 100,000 cells per well. At this stage the cells still have complete blastoma character. Naturally, this line is characterized by epithelial like cells and neuronal like cells occurring simultaneously and spontaneously merging into these two morphological forms. The aim of the differentiation is therefore to promote the neuronal like form and to filter out the epithelial like cells by splitting them 1:1 several times. 1 day after splitting on 6-well plates the switch to differentiation medium 1 takes place. Here the serum content is reduced and additionally cell proliferation is inhibited with 10 µM all-trans retinoic acid. Tretinoin is also used in a subtype of leukaemia because it causes the leukaemia cells to differentiate. Morphologically, no clear change can yet be detected, but proliferation is clearly inhibited, however not stopped. Undifferentiated SHSY5Y cells, day 0 After one week the first 1:1 split is executed onto new 6-well plates. After the cells have been removed, all wells are combined and applied to the new plates at the same volume. The incubation time of the trypsin is deliberately kept somewhat shorter than is usual for the conservation culture, because the epithelial like cells are only replaced later due to their epithelial character. The next day the differentiation medium 2 is used. The amount of tretinoin remains unchanged at 10 µM, while the amount of serum is further reduced to 1%. With the withdrawal of the growth factors, the cells proliferate only sparsely. In this medium they remain only 3 days. Nevertheless, the first changes are clearly visible under the transmitted light microscope. The first real cell extensions form and the differentiation has begun. First signs of beginning differentiation in SHSY5Y, day 10 On the tenth day, the cells are split again 1:1, but this time only incubated at room temperature in order to strain the cells with their fragile cell runners as little as possible. The cells are then applied to 24-well plates coated with 12 µg human fibronectin. The fibronectin was blocked with BSA so that the applied cells did not bind unspecifically. Since the differentiated SHSY5Y also lose the robustness of tumour cells as differentiation progresses, they also need more anchoring and support so that the cell-cell connections that are supposed to form are not damaged during handling or no connections are formed in the first place. On the following day, medium 3 is switched to differentiation. Its basis is no longer EMEM, but neurobasal. The serum content is now completely reduced, instead various neural factors are now contained. Important are dibuturyl cAMP, which acts as a cAMP analogue in intracellular metabolic pathways, and BDNF (brain-derived neurotrophic factor), which in experiments1 already has a significant influence on neurogenesis and the formation and protection of synapses. Supplement B-27 plays a protective role as a radical scavenger. The following days the cells are kept on the coated plates and differentiation medium 3, with medium changing every 3 days. Almost fully differentiated SHSY5Y, day 15 Already after 3 days clear differences and a strong increase of cell-cell connections become visible, which allow a prospect in which direction the differentiation will develop. The strengthening neuronal character is well recognizable. The differentiation is completed after 20 days. 2 days longer than our protocol provides. These are large clusters of cells that look very similar to neuronal primary cell cultures. The accumulated strands of dendritic compounds are also clear, even if it can be assumed that no functioning network has developed. Nevertheless, the morphological transformation of small, irregular tumour cells into huge cell networks spanning the entire well is impressive. In conclusion, it can be said that our differentiation functions excellently. The Synthesis was executed after the following schematic plan. Schematic reprasentation of the stepwise chemical alternation of the eslicarbamizine. The step-to-step execution of the synthesis can be found in our labbook. For the first step of our synthesis, we achieved a satisfying yield after testing two different reaction pathways. Mass spectroscopy, as well as NMR-Analysis, showed that the exchange of the carboxy group with a thiol group has been successful. The yield was determined to be approximately 40%. The second step of the synthesis was executed simultaneously with the synthesis of the azide-donor necessary for the last reaction. After some experiments to alter the reaction, we were able to successfully synthesize the azide-donor. The drying of the azide-donor was critical, as the donor decomposes in vacuo and under mild heat. As it’s a non-explosive and air-stable salt, maybe drying it in a desiccator could be a solution. Nonetheless, the Donor is unstable as long it’s not completely dry and must be handled with care. But by using the sulfate salt, the reaction became much safer. The normaly used chloride salt should not be utilized, and the sulfate salt is an easy to make, shock-resistant alternative. Draft of the sythesis of the azide-donor. After executing the second reaction step the analytic results indicated that no reaction took place. Mass spectroscopy data suggested that after the reaction both reactants were still present in their original form as well as cleavage products of the 2,2'-Dithiodiethylamine. During troubleshooting, we hypothesized that changing the conditions of the reaction would lead to a positive outcome. We considered possible changes could be the reaction time and -temperature. The reaction time might have been too short or the activation energy of the transitional state was too high. A possible solution for the first problem could be to increase the reaction time. For the second problem, it would be possible to increase the reaction temperature. But with the already unstable 2,2'-Dithiodiethylamine, this might just destroy the reactants. A catalysator would be another option to decrease the activation energy. We also considered taking a whole different approach. Because of the alkene as a by-product, the yield was always very small. If the alkene was chosen as the product, targeting it with an S-Nucleophile, the yield could be improved. Plus, the disulfide binding could be produced simply by using a molecule with a disulfide binding. Due to a shortage of time, we were unable to test our possible solutions. Reactionstep two: the reaction was not successful We tried to reobtain the reactants, but the Thio-Eslicarbazepine is not stable in water. The thiol group tends to decay, forming the alkene. As the solvent for the second reaction step was a water- THF-mixture, the Thio-Eslicarbazepine couldn't be reextracted in a sufficient amount. As a result we weren't able to test the Thio-Eslicarbazepine on our neuron-like cells to see if it's still preventing the sodium from entering, which would leave them less excitable. To read more, go to our BioInfo page. Parts pHluorin2 BBa_K2689000 pHluorin2 is a ratiometric, pH-dependent GFP. Its excitation spectrum varies as the pH increases/decreases. This allows pHluorin2 to be used as an accurate biosensor. A special use case is the tracking of proteins that move between different cell compartments and encounter varying pH environments. Compared to pHluorin, pHluorin2 additionally shows higher fluorescence levels. It was developed by Matthew Mahon. While GFP itself shows a relatively stable fluorescence behavior across a wide pH range (at least too stable to be quantified easily), a look at the chromophore suggests that quite a lot of acid-base chemistry is influencing the fluorescence. Because those reactions are confined to the inner core of GFPs beta barrel structure, the solvent pH does not affect the chromophore in a easily quantifiable way. The idea behind pHluorins is to exploit these characteristics and change the structure of GFP in a way that it interfaces the internal reactions to the solvent pH so that it has a direct influence on their fluorescence behavior. For this purpose Miesenböck, et al. performed a directed mutagenesis approach in which they developed two forms of pH-dependent GFPs: Ecliptic and ratiometric pHluorin. These pHluorins show a marked difference in their absorption spectra when encountering different ambient pH. The wild type form of GFP has a bimodal excitation spectrum with two peaks at about 395nm and 475nm. While the ecliptic variants of pHluorin show a decreased fluorescence signal with lower pH, the ratiometric pHluorins show a more complex pattern: With lower pH, the absorption at 395nm decreases while the absorption at 475nm increases. This is a key attribute for using it as a fluorescent pH probe. Syntaxin1A BBa_K2689001 Syntaxins are nervous-specific integral membrane proteins involved in membrane fusion. They possess a C-terminal transmembrane domain, a SNARE domain and an N-terminal regulatory domain. Syntaxin-1A is involved in ion channel regulation and synaptic exocytosis. It interacts with SNAP25 and Synaptobrevin-2 to form the SNARE complex. This complex formation brings vesicle membrane and cell membrane into close proximity, which leads to merging of both membranes. In neuronal cells, synaptic vesicles store neurotransmitters that are released upon stimulation of the cell. This exocytosis process is, among other things, is regulated by Syntaxin1a. Syntaxin-1A is a known substrate of botulinum neurotoxin C, a protein with metalloprotease activity which prevents the exocytosis of neurotransmitters into the neuromuscular junction. Consequently, this leads to flaccid paralysis symptoms of the illness botulism. SNAP25 BBa_K2689002 Synaptosomal-associated Protein 25 (SNAP-25) is a t-SNARE protein involved in the regulation of membrane and vesical fusion. It is integrated into the membrane via palmityl side chains and faces the cytosolic side. Together with Syntaxin-1A and Synaptobrevin-2, it forms the SNARE complex, in which it contributes 2 a-helices. This complex brings together vesicle membrane and cell membrane, which leads to the fusion of both. In neuronal cells, this leads to the exocytosis of neurotransmitters into the interneuronal junction. SNAP-25 is a known substrate of botulinum neurotoxin A, C and E, a protein with metalloprotease activity which prevents the exocytosis of neurotransmitters into the neuromuscular junction. Consequently, this leads to flaccid paralysis symptoms of the illness botulism. Omomyc BBa_K2689003 Omomyc is a dominant-negative mutant of Myc, a family of proto-oncogenes encoding for transcription factors which regulate growth, proliferation, tumorgenesis, and apoptosis. Myc dimerizes with Max over its basic helix-loop-helix zipper domain. This heterodimer then binds to the DNA enhancer box (E-Box) sequence and thus regulates the expression of corresponding genes. In cancer Myc often is constitutively expressed, leading to increased proliferation signals and thus to excessive tumor growth. Omomyc possesses a mutated basic helix-loop-helix zipper domain, in which 4 amino acids were exchanged: S57T, E64I, R70Q and R71N. These mutations lead to an altered dimerization behavior of the protein. Omomyc efficiently forms homodimers and furthermore also forms heterodimers with wild-type Myc, leading to a decreased formation of Myc/Max dimers. Accordingly, Omomyc works as a suppressor of Myc/Max binding to E-box elements and leads to a suppressed activation of artificial E-box promoter elements. In the past it was shown that Omomyc can selectively trigger apoptosis in cells overexpressing Myc, however the exact mechanism remains unclear. Researchers proposed that this happens through the transcriptional repression of specific genes. Through its ability to induce apoptosis in cells overexpressing Myc, as various cancers do, Omomyc may be subject to new therapeutic opportunities in the future. Important Data: Absorption maximum 1: 395 nm Absorption maximum 2: 475 nm Emission maximum: 509 nm When carrying out protein tracking experiments with pHluorin one has no ability to differentiate the cause for a decreasing fluorescence signal. While a possible reason can be a decrease pH, several other effects influence the signal strength. Those can be changing protein concentrations (e.g. caused by degradation), or fluorescence quenching due to long exposure times. To actually identify a decrease in pH, a single signal value is not enough. This is where ratiometric proteins come into play. Rather than using just one signal measurement to estimate the ambient pH, the observer performs two measurements at a different wavelength. Because the preferred excitation wavelength of pHluorin changes with pH, the ratio of the signals is an indicator of pH unaffected by the confounding effects described above. To test the absorption maxima based on pH, we expressed the protein coding sequence in Lemo21 E.coli cells and purified the cells via a HisTrap FF column. For the fluorescence measurement, we used the purified protein in PBS with a buffer capacity of 20 µM. We analogously prepared a PBS buffer with a buffer capacity of 70 µM and titrated it to pH values from 5.3 to 7.5. 80 µl aliquots of the second buffer were given into a White Opaque PerkinElmer CulturPlate-96. For a given pH three samples were prepared as triplets in three separate wells. After that, we added 20 µl of our protein solution (with a concentration of about 0.5mg/ml). We measured fluorescence using a Tecan M200 Infinite Pro plate reader with the following settings: Excitation Scan Excitation Wavelength Start 340 nm Excitation Wavelength End 520 nm Excitation Wavelength Step Size 3 nm Emission Wavelength 550 nm Integration Time 2 0µs Number of flashes 25 Gain 50 This measurement yielded an emission spectrum (averaged over each three wells) for a pH range of 2,2 in 0,3 increments. Figure 3 Figure 3: 415 nm/ 478 nm ratio of total fluorescence counts in dependance to pH, values are presented as quotients of total fluorescence counts Tox Assay ToxAssay To provide a safe work environment for everyone involved in the process of protein purification and BoNTC based assays, we had first to prove, that our mutated botulinum toxin would not be able to cause neurotoxic cleavage of Snap25. Since the BoNTC light chain (LC) alone can not enter a cell, we expressed and purified the LC to test its cleavage ability compared to the wildtype LC. Based on the papers on findings of Vadakkanchery et. al and Edwin J.Vazquez-Cintron et al. we developed an assay which would show the activity difference between the purified LC wildtype compared to our LC mutant. The principle of the assay was the catalytic cleavage of the SNAP25 protein. SNAP25 cleavage by an active LC, which the assay was based on, results in a peptide fragment with the following amino acid sequence: N’ATKMLGSGWSHPQFEK’C (Mpeptide: 1803 [g/mol]). We chose a buffer for our cleavage assay composed of 150mM K+ glutamate and 10mM HEPES, adjusted pH to 7.3 with NaOH. The incubation took place for over 18 hours at 37°C. Analytic samples were drawn from the reaction at the beginning of the assay labeled P0 and after the 18 hours labeled P1. In order to obtain an optimal and unambiguous result, we selected the most sensitive detection methods available, Matrix-assisted laser desorption/ionization (MALDI). According to our knowledge, we were the first to show a successful SNAP25 cleavage assay via MALDI-TOF measurement. MALDI, combined with mass spectrometry, is a special procedure that enables the analysis of peptides, proteins and oligonucleotides through gentle ion generation. The analytical sample is embedded in a suitable matrix, which shows a high absorption at the irradiated laser wavelength, whereby not only higher intensities of the analyte molecule ions are obtained, but almost no fragment ions occur. Since the sensitivity of such mass spectrometric techniques extends into the femtomol and subfemtomol range, this method was selected to provide us with clear results regarding our ToxAssay. This very high accuracy is also reflected in following graphs: As displayed, each time the MALDI shows a peak at 1804 m/z generated through SNAP25 cleavage by LCwt which fits the molecular weight of described peptide. The consistency of peak occurance in an indicator for LC enzymatic activity and maintenance of its protein function. In contrast, none of the samples incubated with LCmut show the described peptide fragment at 1804 m/z, which leads us to the conclusion that this enzyme was successfully deactivated via the triple mutation and thus leads in combination with the heavy chain to an inactivated, nontoxic botulinum neurotoxin. To make sure, we detected the correct cleavage product, we further validated our results using a peptide synthesized specifically for this purpose as a positive control with the aforementioned peptide sequence. The peak displayed when only measuring the synthetic peptide, proved to be exactly the peak seen in the samples incubated with LCwt and therefore fortifies our conclusion of a successful detoxification.
