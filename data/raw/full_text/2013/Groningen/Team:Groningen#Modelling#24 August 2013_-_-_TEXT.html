Where X= Ta*x1*x2 + x1*T0 and Y =x3(VP+TM1+TD0). Where x2 determines the increase in receptor activity when a stimulatory ligand is bound (Ta) with respect to an unbound receptor (T0). x1 is a scaling factor that ensures that the maximum increase for k5 is within some predetermined range. x3 is also a scaling factor, which ensures that the negative feedback determined by VP, TM1 and TD0 concentrations can potentially compensate for the positive increase. The error function strived to minimize the difference between k5 values when 0 attractant was present, with respect to when 10+ attractant is present. The idea is that the increase in receptor activity by stimulatory ligands is then, over time, fully compensated for by the negative feedback, as is the case in the real chemotaxis system.
