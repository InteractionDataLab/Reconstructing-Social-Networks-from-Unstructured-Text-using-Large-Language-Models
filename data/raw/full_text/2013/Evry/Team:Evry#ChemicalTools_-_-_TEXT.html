EntA, B and C are involved in the conversion of chorismic acid into 2,3-dihydroxybenzoic acid (DHB). EntD, E and F catalyze the amide linkage of DHB to L-serine. Three molecules of the DHB-Serine are required for an intermolecular cyclization, thus yielding enterobactin. To conlude, the production of those 6 enzymes can thus become a rate-limiting-step, when it comes to mass enterobactin production.
