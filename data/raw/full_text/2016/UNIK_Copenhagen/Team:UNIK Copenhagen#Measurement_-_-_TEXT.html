Measuring sucrose with HPAEC-PAD Sucrose is a disaccharide made from up of a unit of glucose and fructose with a glycosyl bond between them. To accurately measure it, we have applied Dionex- High-performance anion exchange chromatography - pulsed amperometric detection (HPAEC-PAD). The amount of sucrose secreted in the media was determined using Dionex- High-performance anion exchange chromatography - pulsed amperometric detection (HPAEC-PAD) by using CarboPac SA10 (Thermo Scientific) column for fast separation of sugars. HPAEC-PAD is an analytical device to detect and characterise simple to complex carbohydrates in the sample based on their electrocatalytic activity at high pH-level using NaOH. 200 µL of inoculated spinned media containing secreted sucrose was collected twice a day from the first day of induction for 7 days. The spent media was transferred to a HPAEC-PAD Vial (and septra) and the running program was consisted by a constant flow rate of 0,35 mL/min with the injection volume of 20 µL.
