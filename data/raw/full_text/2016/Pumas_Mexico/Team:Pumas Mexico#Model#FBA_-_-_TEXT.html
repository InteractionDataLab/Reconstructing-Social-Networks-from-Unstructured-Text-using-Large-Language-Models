The metabolic reconstruction we used was that of Chlorella vulgaris UTEX 395, published on July of this year 2. This reconstruction is based on Chlamydomonas reinhardii, with experimental and bioinformatic data on the literature. It includes 843 out of 7,100 annotated genes (around 12%), delineating 1,770 metabolites and 2,294 reactions, being the amino acid and lipid metabolisms the most accurately reconstructed 2. Importantly the authors of this model report that the uptake rates were experimentally verified experimentally. First, we analyzed which light wavelength was the best for the overall growth of the algae according to the metabolic model. When comparing different type of lights in the metabolic model, we couldn't find a notorious difference between the different light sources. It's important to emphasize that this only happens if we find and apply the optimal value in each of the conditions. Spectral decomposition of solar light measured from outer space is the first studied condition. In this case we find the solution that optimized best our model, more specifically the one that increases the overall production. Also we tried the effect of oxygen, given that it is present in small quantities, because we were not able to get a sealed biorreactor.
