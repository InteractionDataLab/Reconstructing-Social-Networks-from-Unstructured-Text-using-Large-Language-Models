The pH of cells grown with both light and B-carotene rose more slowly than other cultures, suggesting that the plasmid could be used to moderate the alkalinity of an MEC cathode and provide protons for hydrogen production. This is in agreement with previous characterization performed by UNITN-Trento 2015. The dissolved oxygen concentration of liquid cultures of E. coli BL21 was compared for wild type cells and those transformed with nox plasmid K3282003. The presence of the nox plasmid lowered the dissolved oxygen concentration over time in cultures with the same number of cells exposed to air.
