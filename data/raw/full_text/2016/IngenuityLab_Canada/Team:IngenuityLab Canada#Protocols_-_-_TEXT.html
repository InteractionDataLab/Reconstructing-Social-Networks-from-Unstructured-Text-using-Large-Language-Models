Add 10ml of stock kanamycin (sterile filtered) into the filtered 9.99 L of media. Recovery of HT3 cells from glycerol stocks Obtain HT3 cells stored in HT3 Media + 8% DMSO form nitrogen storage. Thaw the cells in 42˚C water bath. After thawing is completed, transfer the culture to Eppendorf Tube. Centrifuge for 3 min at 7000 rpm using table top centrifuge. Discard the Supernatant and re-suspend the cells in 1mL of freshly prepared HT3 media. Inoculate the cells from Step 5 in 49 mL of the HT3 Media in a 250 mL flask using sterile techniques. Incubate the flask in dark (tin foil wrap) at 30˚C at 100 rpm shaker overnight. After 24 hours, incubate the 50 mL culture in photosynthetic light 50 μmol/m2s and incubate at 250 rpm. Measure Absorbance of the culture daily at 730 nm in a 1 ml Cuvette. After the cells have Optical Density of 0.8, inoculate the 50 mL culture into freshly prepared 450 mL of HT3 Media in a 1 L flask. Incubate at 30ᵒC at 250 rpm under 50 μmol/m2s photosynthetic light. Monitor the Optical Density as in Step 9 daily and once reached 0.8 the culture is ready to be inoculated in 10L of media. Inoculation and Growing of 10 L of HT3 Media Once the 500 mL cultures in 1 L flask have reached Optical Density of >0.8, inoculate 200 mL of culture into 10L of HT3 Media. Place the Carboy into the incubator at 30OC with multiple Photosynthetic light surrounding carboy. Feed filtered (0.2-micron Sorbitol Filter) air into the carboy to induce bubbling and stirring. Measure the Optical Density daily. Once the Optical density has reached 1.0, the culture is ready is to be harvest to purify the Photosystem II protein. Cryopreservation of HT3 Cells Prepare HT3 Media with 8% DMSO. Centrifuge 1 mL of Cell culture with Optical Density of 0.8 at 7000 rpm for 3 min. Re-suspend the cells in 1 mL of HT3 Media containing 8% DMSO. Place the re-suspended cells in Cyro-freezer compatible tubes. Store the cells in Cyrofreezer in Liquid Nitrogen for long term storage. Photosystem II Purification Culture Harvest Grow HT3 cells in BG-11 media supplemented with glucose and Kanamycin in 10L carboy under 50 uE m-2s- 1 at 30ᵒC with air bubbling for 3 days. Check the Optical Density (OD) at 730nm and harvest only when the OD is >0.8 Harvest culture using the Floor-model centrifuge; cell pellets by re-suspending them with Buffer A (50mM MES-NaOH, pH 6.0, 10mM MgCl2, 5 mM CaCl2, 25% glycerol) Combine washed cell pellets and centrifuge at 12,000gs for 10 minutes. Resuspend cells in Buffer A; Determine chlorophyll A (chlA) concentration with 80% (v/v) acetone and check the Oxygen Evolving Activity. Collect the PSII sample and aliquot in PCR tubes to be stored in -80ᵒC Cyrofreezer Proteoliposomes Preparation Making Liposomes: In a glass vial measure out Escherichia coli Total Lipid Extract and dissolve it in chloroform to a concentration of 40mg lipid /ml solvent stock From the stock, transfer a volume of the solution mix to another glass vial and mix with chloroform to a concentration of 20mg/ml. For a small volume of 2ml or less of the 20mg/ml, evaporate the chloroform using dry nitrogen gas in a gas fume hood until there a thin lipid film. for larger volumes, use round bottom flask in rotatory evaporator to prepare a thin lipid film. Rehydrate the lipid film from step 3 by suspending the lipid film in buffer (5mM MES, 50mM KCl, 200µM Pyranine, 2mM MgCl2 pH 6.5) to a final concentration of 15.34mg/ml. Vortex until the film emulsifies into the buffer Sonicate the emulsified solution for 5-10 minutes. Freeze the emulsified solution for 5 minutes at -80ᵒC. Transfer the solution to a 42ᵒC water bath for 5 minutes; Repeat (Step 7-8), the freeze-thaw cycle twice Using Avanti Polar Lipid Extruder, extrude through a 0.4µm nucleopore polycarbonate membrane 21 times to form liposomes. The liposome can be stored in -80ᵒC until needed for use. Preparing the Proteoliposome: Mix buffer (5mM MES, 50mM KCl, 2mM MgCl2, 2mM CaCl2 pH 6.52) and liposome solution to a concentration of 4mg/ml, Add detergent at saturated (Rsat) and solubilized (Rsol) concentration. For CHAPS, Rsat is 3.0mM and Rsol at 7.0mM Photosystem II protein concentration to 2.3mg/ml, Incubate the detergent and liposome mix overnight (~10 hours) Add photosystem II protein to the lipid-detergent solution and incubate for 30 minutes at 4ᵒC mixing gently on a rotating mixer. Keep all the samples in dark from this point on. Detergent Removal Step: After protein-lipid-detergent incubation, add 80mg of BiobeadsTM suspeneded in buffer and incubate for 1 hour at 4ᵒC on the rotator). 20grams of BiobeadsTM SM-2 Resin dissolved in buffer (5mM MES, 50mM KCl, 2mM MgCl2, 2mM CaCl2 pH 6.52), degassed and stored in 4ᵒC for use. Repeat (Step 14) for 3 detergent additions; after the third addition, add 240mg of BiobeadsTM. Remove the BiobeadsTM from the proteoliposomes by SPN Column Protein Assay and centrifuge in bench-top micro-centrifuge at 1000g for 1 minutes to separate BiobeadsTM from solution. Removing Pyranine from Assay: Desalt the proteoliposomes on the FPLC using a 5ml Hi-trap column in buffer (5mM MES, 50mM KCl, 2mM MgCl2, 2mM CaCl2 pH 6.52) Pool fractions in the center 90% of the target peak. (1mL per well) Oxygen Activity: Run Oxygen Evolution on the liposome (refer to the oxygen evolution protocol. Mix by pipetting Stored in -80˚C for 1 hour. To allow for DNA precipitation Centrifuge at 15,000 x g for 30 minutes at 4˚C. To pellet the precipitated DNA Decant the supernatant, carefully, using a pipette tip. Do not disturb the DNA pellet Wash the DNA pellet twice, using 70% ethanol. Be careful not to disturb the pellet Air dry at room temperature to evaporate residual 70% ethanol Re-dissolve your DNA pellet in suitable buffer solution or neutral pH deionized water that is nuclease-free Isopropanol precipitation Adjust the salt concentration using sodium acetate (NaOAc, 0.3M, pH 5.2, final concentration) Add 1 volumes of 100% isopropanol.
