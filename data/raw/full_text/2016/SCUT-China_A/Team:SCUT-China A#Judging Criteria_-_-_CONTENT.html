Requirements for a Bronze Medal：

     We registered the team, had a great summer, and planed to have fun at the Giant Jamboree.

     Meet all deliverables on the Requirements page (section 3).

     We created a page on our team wiki with clear attribution of each aspect of our project. This page must clearly attribute work done by the students and distinguish it from work done by others, including host labs, advisors, instructors, sponsors, professional website designers, artists, and commercial services. (For more details, click here)

     We documented at least one new standard BioBrick Part or Device central to our project and submited these part to the iGEM Registry .

Requirements for a Silver Medal:

     We Experimentally validate that our new BioBrick Part of our own design and construction works as expected.

     We have helped two registered iGEM team from other universities in a significant way. (For more details, click here)

     Our iGEM project involved and considered important questions beyond the bench. (For more details, click here)

Requirements for a Gold Medal:

     We expand on our silver medal activity by demonstrating how we had integrated the investigated issues into the design and/or execution of our project. (For more details, click here)

     We demonstrated a functional proof of concept of our project. (For more details, click here)

     We showed our project working under real-world conditions. To achieve this criterion, we demonstrated our whole system working under simulated conditions in the lab. (For more details, click here)

Therefore, we believe that we deserve a Gold Medal Prize.
