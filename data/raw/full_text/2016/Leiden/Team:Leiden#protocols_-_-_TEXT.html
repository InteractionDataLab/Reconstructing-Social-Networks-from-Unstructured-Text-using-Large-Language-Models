Team:Leiden/protocols iGEM Leiden Wiki / Protocols Protocols Plasmid isolation by alkaline lysis This protocol tells you how to isolate plasmids from a liquid cell culture using alkaline lysis. Materials Ice box with ice Solution 1: Tris-HCl, EDTA, RNase (4°C; for RNase stability) Solution 2: NaOH, SDS (Room temperature) Solution 3: KOAc (4°C;) Isopropanol or 100% ethanol (-20°C;) Bacterial cultures of choice Protocol Transfer 1.5 mL of cell culture to an Eppendorf tube. Centrifuge 2 minutes at 13,400 rpm and pour off the supernatant. Add 200 µL of solution 1 to the pellet. Vortex/resuspend the pellet thoroughly. Add 400 µL of solution 2 and mix carefully by inverting. Add 300 µL of ice cold solution 3 and mix by shaking. Put the mixture on ice for 5 minutes. Centrifuge 5 minutes at 13,400 rpm. Transfer the SUPERNATANT (!) to a new eppendorf tube - do not throw it away! Add 600 µL of ice cold isopropanol or 100% ethanol and put the mixture on ice for 5 minutes. Centrifuge for 2 minutes at 13,400 rpm. Pipette the supernatant off and place the eppendorf tubes with open lids in a rack to dry the pellet. If dry, the pellet should be resuspended in, for example, 50 µl demiwater. Transformation Materials Table centrifuge Plates (with antibiotics if needed) Competent cells Ice Waterbath (42°C;) SOC/LB medium (SOC preffered) Protocol Put competent cells from -80°C; on ice. Leave on ice for 30 minutes. Add all of your ligation mix (or x amount of whatever mix you want to transform)to the cells. Keep cells as much on ice as possible, flick the cells after adding mix to ensure homologous distribution. Leave 30 minutes on ice. Heat shock the cells. Do this 30-90 seconds (30 seconds is fine) at 42°C.; Important is to ensure the cells experience actual 42°C;, use a waterbath for this and swirl the tubes in the water during heat shock. Put 2 minutes on ice. Add 950 µl SOC medium and grow at 37°C; and 250rpm for 45-60 minutes. Note: we use big shaker on 150 rpm for this, no shaking heat block available. Note: you can also use LB instead of SOC (SOC is preffered). Plate 2 plates, 1 with 50 µl; culture and 1 with the rest of your culture ~1ml. Make sure you have the appropriate antibiotics in your plates. To plate 1ml of culture I spin down the cells (after plating all 50 µl plates) in table centrifuge at 4500 rpm for 2 minutes. Decant the supernatant (not everything!) to leave around 50-100 µl of LB/SOC, resuspend and plate. Put in stove 37°C; for O/N
