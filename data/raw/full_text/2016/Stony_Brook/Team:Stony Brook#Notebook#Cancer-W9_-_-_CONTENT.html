    8/26

    Met with Dr. Zainul about our project

    • Test would have to be administered in a lab
    • Only on an at risk population (family history, heavy alcohol consumption, etc.)
    • Would need to get the protocol down and license to sell it to a lab
    • Not all private practices have centrifuges, so doing the test with a visually detectable indicator won't work
    • Not all private practices have UV lights, so the draw of this being fast may not be as great as we thought
    • Maybe consider light chromatography
    • If this can be done in a private practice, we'll need a sort of stick test, similar to the glucose and protein tests for urine
    • Can consider coupling GPC-1 binding to secretion of a selected product that can bind to an external indicator that can be poured into the solution, like phenolphthalein
