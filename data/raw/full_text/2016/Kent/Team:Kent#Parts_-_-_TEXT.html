The majority of the parts were cloned into the pSB1C3 backbone using either Gibson assembly or restriction enzyme ligations. Some of parts were cloned into pSB1A3 because a co-transformation with a chloramphenicol resistant plasmid containing a cytochrome maturation factor is required for the parts to function, or because they needed to be transformed into VS45 E. coli strain that also contains a chloramphenicol resistant plasmid. The genes were initially cloned into plasmids containing an RFP indicator (BBa_JJ04450), which allowed for easy visualisation of correct colonies.
