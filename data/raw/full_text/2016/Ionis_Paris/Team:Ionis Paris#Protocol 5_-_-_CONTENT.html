    Protocol 5 : Preparation of competent cells

    Aim: Prepare competent bacteria for subsequent transformation

    Inoculation

    Inoculate 100µL of cells in 100mL LB medium Let the culture grow O/N at 37°C

    CaCl2 preparation

    Put 1.11g of CaCl2 in 100mL of sterile H2O to obtain a final concentration of 0.1M of CaCl2 (Molar mass of Cacl2: 110,98g/mol)

    Competency

    Inoculate 3mL of the overnight culture cell from in 90mL of LB. Shake at 37°C until the DO is around 0,6 NB : all the following steps must be perform in ice Transfer cells into sterile ice-cold Falcon tubes Incubate on ice for 30 min Spin cells at 4000 rpm for 10 min at 6°C Discard supernatant and try to drain all remaining media Gently resuspend in 10mL cold 0,1M CaCl2 Incubate on ice 20 min Centrifuge 10 min at 4000 rpm at 4°C Discard supernatant and vortex, gently resuspend on 5mL cold 0,1M CaCl2/15% Glycerol Transfer in 2mL cryotubes Store at -80°C for subsequent transformation

    Reagents

    • E.coli DH5α or BL21
    • LB medium (see Protocol 1 : Culture medium preparation)
    • CaCl2 (Panreac, 131232)
    • Glycerol (Sigma-Aldrich - G5516)

    Materials

    • Sterile 1.5mL tubes
    • 50mL falcon tube (VWR, 525-0156)
    • Cryotube (Dutscher 055003)
    • Spectrophotometer
    • Incubator
    • Centrifuge
    • Ice bucket
