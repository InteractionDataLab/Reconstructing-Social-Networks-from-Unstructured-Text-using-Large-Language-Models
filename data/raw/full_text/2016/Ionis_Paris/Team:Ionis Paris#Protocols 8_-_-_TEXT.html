Pick a colony and add a stab of it into a PCR tube. Gently mix and spin down microcentrifuge. Transfer PCR tubes to a PCR machine with the block preheated to 95°C Set the following parameters for the PCR reaction : Lid Temperature: 95°C Initial denaturation : 95°C 30 s 25 cycles of : - 95°C 30s - 58°C 60s - 68°C; 1 min per kB (amplification) Final extension : 68°C for 5 min Hold at 4°C Reagents 10X Taq rection buffer (NEB, #B9014S) 10 mM dNTPs (NEB, #N0447S) 10 µM Forward Primer 10 µM Reverse Primer Template DNA (1-2µg) Taq DNA Polymerase (NEB, #M0273) Nuclease-free water Materials Thermocycler(Bio Rad) 0,2 mL PCR tubes (VWR, 732-0548) Sterile 1.5mL microcentrifuge tubes (Eppendorf, 0030,123,328) Sources Colony PCR Protocol (Penn iGEM 2013) Facebook Twitter Flickr LinkedIn Dribble Vimeo More about us If you’ve followed along and done the steps above, you’ve created a good starting place.
