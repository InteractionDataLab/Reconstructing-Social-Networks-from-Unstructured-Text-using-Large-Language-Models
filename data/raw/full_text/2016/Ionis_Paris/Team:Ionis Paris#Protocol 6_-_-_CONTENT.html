    Protocol 6 : E.Coli Dh5α and BL21 transformation (Heat-shock)

    Aim: Introduce plasmid DNA in E.Coli bacteria

    Thaw a tube of DH5-alpha competent cells on ice for 10 min. Mix gently and carefully pipette 50 µL of cells into a transformation tube on ice Add 1-5µL plasmid DNA to the cell mixture (one tube for each ratio) Carefully flick the tube 4-5 times to mix cells and DNA. Do not vortex. Place the mixture on ice for 30 min. Do not mix. Heat shock at exactly 42°C for 45 s. Do not mix. Place on ice for 5 min. Do not mix. Pipette 250µL of room temperature SOC into the mixture. Place at 37°C for 60 min at 250 rpm. Warm selection plates to 25°C. Mix the cells by flicking the tube and inverting. Spread 100µL of cells onto selection plates. Incubate all the plates O/N at 37°C.

    Reagents

    • Competent cells (see Protocol 6)
    • Plasmid DNA
    • SOC medium (NEB, Ref B9020)
    • LB + antibiotic plates (see Protocol 1)

    Materials

      Sterile 1.5mL microcentrifuge tubes (Eppendorf, 0030,123,328)
    • Water bath or dry heater
    • Incubator
