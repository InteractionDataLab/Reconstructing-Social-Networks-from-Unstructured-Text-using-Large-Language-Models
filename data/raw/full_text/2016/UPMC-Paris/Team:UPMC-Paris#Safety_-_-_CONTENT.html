    • Summary :

    In IGEM, safety is taken seriously. In the lab we have used some dangerous products such as gel red for the electrophoresis, so we had special consideration regarding safety issues.

    Safety Training and Lab Rules :

    For our project we safely used strains of Bacillus subtilis and Escherichia coli.All researchers and experiments have been trained in applicable lab safety to insure that no bacteria are inadvertently released into the environment.During either internships or through classes we had at school, we received proper training on safety in the lab. Before we started our experiments for IGEM competition, we received as a team an additional safety training, after what each of us received a detailed video on safety in the lab in case we wanted to refresh our memory. Our safety training included topics such as:

    1. Rules about protocols in the lab,
    2. Protective clothing and equipment (lab coat and if necessary, gloves and googles),
    3. Waste management (biohazard, defiled glassware, …),
    4. Chemicals products handling and their conservation according to their labels,
    5. Use of various machines (open bench, chemical fume hood, ...) and
    6. Emergency procedures.

    So, we learned measures to take in case of spills, burns, injury, different types of danger in the lab, conditions to safely perform experiments and keep the lab area clean for the sake of everybody’s experiments.

    Biological components :

    None of the biological components worked with for iGEM competion were rated higher than biosafety level 1. Nevertheless, proper exposure precautions were still taken in the lab while manipulating reagents such as GelRed, or reading fels with UV light.
