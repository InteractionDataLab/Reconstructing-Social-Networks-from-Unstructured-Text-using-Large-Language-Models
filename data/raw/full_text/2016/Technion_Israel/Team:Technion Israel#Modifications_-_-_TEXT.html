The main goal of project S.Tar was to design new chemoreceptors that do not exist in nature. These new chemoreceptors were based on the Tar chemoreceptor. Said chemoreceptor structure consists of three main regions: Ligand binding domain (LBD), The HAMP domain and the MH bundle (1) Fig. 1: Tar chemoreceptor structure. Our project focused on altering the LBD region by using different tools and approaches each designed to achieve a unique response. The three major approaches were as follows: 1. Tar-Chimeras: a hybrid receptor consisting of an LBD from different sources, such as human and other bacteria, fused to the HAMP and MH of Tar. An example of these hybrids: PctA-Tar and Narx-Tar. 2. Intein: A protein fused into the LBD that functions as an on/off mechanism that allows the control of chemotaxis. 3. Rosseta: a computer software that helps with the design of new LBDs that recognizes the ligand of your choice. Referances 1. BI, Shuangyu; LAI, Luhua. Bacterial chemoreceptors and chemoeffectors.
