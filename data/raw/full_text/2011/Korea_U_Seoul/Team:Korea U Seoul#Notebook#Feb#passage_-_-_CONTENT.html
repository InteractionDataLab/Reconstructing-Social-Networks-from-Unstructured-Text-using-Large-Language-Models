2/16 - The basics

Presentation at 2nd meeting

We had a second meeting to study about iGEM registry.

  • We learned about:
  1. A reason for using low # in plasmid backbone's operation, and its merits and demerits.
  2. How to express parts of plasmid such as RBS, promoter, ...etc.
  3. Types of restriction enzymes
  4. Why a scar occurs and how to prevent it (Silver's method, application of Berkley's method, Lim standard)
  5. How to construct a backbone having desired origins and antibiotic resistance based on Reshma method.
  6. Practical usage in companies.
  7. Both parts and plasmids can be archived (recording, saving, storing)
  • What we didn't understand were:
  1. Why scars occur, and preventing shift caused from it, and a relationship between mixed and scar.
  2. What the meaning of 'shuffling protein domain' and 'overhanging of 4 base pair' in Lims standard are.
  3. Relationship between restriction sites E, S, N.

2/23 - Groups formed

We finished studying the 'Help' section of registry.

  • What we learned and did:
  1. Termination, rho-dependent/independent pathway, types of RNA polymerase.
  2. Design, components, construction, efficiency of terminator.
  3. There are forward, reverse, and bidirectional terminators.
  4. How to differentiate between natural parts and synthesized parts using barcode.
  5. Parts symbols.
  6. Read dissertations related to the above topics.
  • Questions
  1. The relationship between poly (A) tract, poly (T) tract and bidirectional terminator
  2. (http://partsregistry.org/Help:Terminators/Design)
  3. What is a reverse primer and why we need to design it in oligo terminator construction
  4. (http://partsregistry.org/Help:Terminators/Construction)
  5. The meaning of forward/bidirectional/reverse terminator
  6. The Existence of forward/bidirectional/reverse terminator in eukaryotic cell
  7. The meaning of barcode in forward/bidirectional/reverse terminator
  8. The meaning of stop/start/binding symbols

  3. Explanation of oscillators based on ZNFs
  4. Peking
  5. Heavy metal decontamination kit; detection of mercury and its coagulation.
  6. BCCS-Bristol
  7. Measuring of nitrogen concentration using microorganism to measure soil fertility level.
