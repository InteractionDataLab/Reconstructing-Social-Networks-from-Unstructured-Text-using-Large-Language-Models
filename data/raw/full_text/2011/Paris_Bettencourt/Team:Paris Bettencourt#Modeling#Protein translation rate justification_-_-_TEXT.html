In our case, because our concentrations are in molecules per cell, we use directly this number in our models. We took the average distance between ribosomes (around 45 nucleotides [1] ) and divided it by its average translation speed on mRNA (15 codon.s-1 [2] ) and we obtained the maximum translation rate of mRNA by ribosomes around 1 protein.s-1. Since our constructs are typically 1kB long and it is commonly admitted the ribosome makes a translation error roughly every 10 000 nucleotides, we assumed there was a 10% error in the translation of our genes. Our protein translation rate ( ) is therefore 0.9 protein.s-1.
