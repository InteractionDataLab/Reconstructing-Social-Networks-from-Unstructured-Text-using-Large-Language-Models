Roots secrete a variety of compounds that E. coli are not attracted to naturally. Accordingly, we engineered a chemoreceptor into our chassis that can sense malate, a common root exudate, so that it can swim towards the root. Additionally, E. coli are actively taken up by plant roots, which will allow targeted IAA delivery into roots by our system. Overview Specifications Design Modelling Assembly Testing & Results Future Work Assembly We have chosen to test two chemoreceptors. McpS from P. putida and PA2652 from P. aeruginosa. In order to test the receptor´s ability to rewire chemotaxis towards malate, we had to assemble the construct first. We have codon optimised the sequences for E. coli and B. subtilis. Then we have ordered the sequences from Eurofins. Both mcpS and PA2652 are long constructs (1923bp & 1689bp respectively, just for the coding sequence), therefore we have decided to get it synthesised in two fragments. The assembly into the vector pSB1C3 would follow one of the multifragment assembly strategies. One modification was made to the existing assembly strategies such as CPEC or Gibson, which have removed the first PCR step from the set of assembly steps that are required to follow. This has decreased the probability of the synthesised fragments mutating due to extensive use of PCR during assembly. The modification itself is based on the addition of MlyI restriction sites at the 5´ & 3´ end of the ordered fragments. Upon digestion with MlyI, which acts as a blunt end endonuclease that cuts five bp away from its recognition site, the synthesised fragment of interest can be gel extracted without having to utilise PCR for fragment isolation from the vector it has been delivered in. In parallel to having codon optimised genes synthesised we have asked Dr Juan Luis Ramos, who has been working with mcpS gene and was at the discovery of its properties as chemoreceptor towards malate, to provide us with this gene. He has kindly agreed to help us and we have obtained from him a non codon optimised mcpS gene with unknown promoter and RBS in a vector pRK415 with tetracycline resistance. Due to short timeframe we were operating on, Eurofins did not manage to synthesise mcpS fragments in time, and therefore we were not able to assemble these. However PA2652 gene fragments have been delivered on time and they have been assembled into pSB1C3 vector using CPEC assembly.
