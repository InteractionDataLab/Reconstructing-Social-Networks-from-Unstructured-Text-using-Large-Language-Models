    Home made quick saliva DNA extraction protocol:

    1. Rinse the mouth 5 times with clean water, the last rinse must be deposited in a plastic glass
    2. Prepare a solution with soap and water in a beaker
    3. Add the soap solution to the last rinse
    4. Add frozen (0°C) etanol to the last rinse
    5. Observe how a DNA thread forms, take it and place it in an eppendorf tube.

    Abbreviations and glossary:

    • Biobrickoatl:It's the biobrick deity for the Mesoamerican cultures, although at that time they didn't knew what a biobrick meant. The great feathered biobrick serpent vigilates over all new and old biobricks.
    • Competent cells: Cells that can incorporate, in an easier way, foreign DNA into their cell walls.
    • CcaS-EnvZ: Is the green light receptor that induces the expresión of phycobilisome linker protein.
    • CFP: Cyan Fluorescent Protein
    • Crst: Lock for the PbadST (strong, for low concentrations) promoter
    • CrWk: Lock for the Pbad WK (weak, for high concentration) promoter
    • GFP: Green Fluorescent Protein
    • ho1: Heme oxigenase 1
    • iTaST: Inverse TaST, complementary sequence to the Key TaST, blocks TaST activity
    • pBADST: Strong arabinose promoter for low concentration detection.
    • pBADWk: Weak arabinose promoter for high concentration detection.
    • Pcy4A: Phycocyanobilin ferredoxin oxidoreductase.
    • RBS: Ribosome binding site
    • TaST: Key for the CrST lock
    • TaWk: Key for the CrWk lock
    • Term: Terminator, not the robot (genetic terminator).
