      Mikael Rørdam AndersenAbout Mikael: Mikael is an associate professor at the Center for Microbial Biotechnology, DTU. He knows everything there is to know about mammalian cells and Aspergilli. Mikael has a very unique but annoying skill - he knows everything that goes on in our building, which means that we can never surprise him with latest results, because he already knows it. Mikael is also our team mascot and goes under the name; Mojo Jojo!
    DTU CV:
    2011- present: Associate Professor, DTU.
    2008-2011: Postdoctoral Researcher, DTU.
    2005-2008: Ph.D in Biotechnology, DTU.
    1999-2005: M.sc.Eng in Biotechnology, DTU.
      Helene Faustrup Kildegaard

    About Helene:  Helene is the magical guardian of the mammalian laboratory. She is a patient and highly skilled supervisor that has provided the team with valuable knowledge of her experiences with mammalian cells. She guards the secret chamber, where the precious mammalian cells live, and only a lucky few are allowed to pass.

    DTU CV: 2009-present: Postdoctoral Researcher, DTU. 2006-2009: Ph.D in Cellular on Molecular biology at DTU in collaboration with Danish Cancer Society. 1999-2005: M.sc.Eng in Biotechnology, DTU.

      Dorte Koefoed Holm

    About Dorte:  Dorte is our fungal expert, and probably the best organized researcher ever seen. Dorte has unique skills in getting things to work - at least anything that's moldy. Dorte makes an excellent penguin, and she has won great awards for this accomplishment. DTU CV: 2010-2013: Ph.D in Biotechnology, DTU. 2005-2010: M.sc.Eng in Biotechnology, DTU.
