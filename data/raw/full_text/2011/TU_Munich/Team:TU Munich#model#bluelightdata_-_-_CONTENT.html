1<a id='magicparlabel-35866' ></a>Model

<a id='magicparlabel-35867' ></a>The blue light sensor was modeled with help of the model by the iGEM team KU Leuven 2009[<a href='#kuleuven'>1</a>]. For further details see the Papers by Tschowri [<a href='#Tschowri-2009'>4</a>] and Nakasone [<a href='#Nakasone-2007'>3</a>, <a href='#Nakasone-2010'>2</a>].
<a id='magicparlabel-37823' ></a>The model does not take into account that the YcgE/YcgF pathway is strongly temparature dependent.
<a id='magicparlabel-35868' ></a>The dye output was an adaption of the model proposed in the paper by Yildirim[<a href='#Yildirim-2004'>5</a>]

5<a id='magicparlabel-36579' ></a>Simulation

<a id='magicparlabel-36580' ></a>The goal of the simulation was to investigate the dependence of the of the activation time and deactivation time as well as the output of dye on the irradiation time and the irradiation intesity.
<a id='magicparlabel-36581' ></a>The range of both were adapted such that further increase would lead to no qualitative change.
<a id='magicparlabel-36929' ></a>
<a id='magicparlabel-36932' ></a><img style='width:100%;' src='https://static.igem.org/mediawiki/2011/9/9e/6_Users_schaluck_Desktop_bactivation.png' alt='image: 6_Users_schaluck_Desktop_bactivation.png' ></a>
<a id='magicparlabel-36933' ></a>
<a id='magicparlabel-39629' ></a>We see that the activation time only depends on the exposure time, not on the intensity which should not coincide with the real world behavior.
<a id='magicparlabel-39630' ></a>The deactivation time is defined as the timespan that the mRNA concentration needs to again drop below the threshold of <math xmlns='http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML'><mrow><mrow><mn>0.5</mn><mi>n</mi><mi>M</mi></mrow></mrow></math> after expiration of the exposure time.
<a id='magicparlabel-36912' ></a>
<a id='magicparlabel-36915' ></a><img style='width:100%;' src='https://static.igem.org/mediawiki/2011/1/15/7_Users_schaluck_Documents_MATLAB_BT_bdeactivation.png' alt='image: 7_Users_schaluck_Documents_MATLAB_BT_bdeactivation.png' ></a>
<a id='magicparlabel-39563' ></a>We see that the deactivation time reaches its upper bound quite fast with respect to both intensity and exposure time. This is due to the fact that the reaction is quite fast and that the two steady states of the bistable behavior are affected by neither intensity nor exposure time. Hence more time is needed to dephosporelate all OmpR and drop the mRNA concentration below the threshold.
<a id='magicparlabel-39564' ></a>The final point of interest is the total output of dye. For this the value of <math xmlns='http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML'><mrow><mrow><msub><mrow><mi>x</mi></mrow><mrow><mn>10</mn></mrow></msub><mo form='prefix' fence='true' stretchy='true' symmetric='true'>(</mo><mn>40000</mn><mo form='postfix' fence='true' stretchy='true' symmetric='true'>)</mo></mrow></mrow></math> was used. Although this might not be the final output of the system, it should be a rough approximation.
<a id='magicparlabel-37448' ></a>
<a id='magicparlabel-37462' ></a><img style='width:100%;' src='https://static.igem.org/mediawiki/2011/f/f4/8_Users_schaluck_Documents_MATLAB_BT_boutput.png'></a>
<a id='magicparlabel-37463' ></a>
<a id='magicparlabel-39670' ></a>Again the output reaches its steady state quite fast in respect to both variables for the same reasons as stated above.

6<a id='magicparlabel-39487' ></a>Conclusion

<a id='magicparlabel-41038' ></a>We can observe that the system reaches its steady states quite fast for sufficiently high intensity and exposure time. For our final system the deactivation time defines the minimum recommended time between two single excitation of focused points. Hence maximum intensity and minimal exposure time is desirable. This behavior does not coincide with our Miller Assays (See <a href='https://2011.igem.org/Team:TU_Munich/team/notebook'>Labbook</a> Cloning III for details).
