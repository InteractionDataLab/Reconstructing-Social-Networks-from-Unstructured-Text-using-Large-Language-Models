For standard ligations use 50 ng of vector DNA, increase the amounts of DNA if unsuccessful. $$Insert\:Mass\:in\:ng = 3\times \bigg[\frac{Insert\:Length\:in\:bp}{Vector\:Length\:in\:bp}\bigg] \times Vector\:Mass\:in\:ng$$ Prepare the ligation mixture: - required amount of insert DNA - required amount of vector DNA - 1 ul of T4 ligase - 2 ul of 10 x ligase buffer - add milliQ H2O up to 20 ul Incubate at 16C for 30 minutes Heat inactivate by incubating at 80C for 20 minutes Keep on ice until ready to proceed with transformation protocol Transformation (If using part from the distribution: resuspend the DNA in 10 ul of MiliQ water, making sure that it turns red. Wait 10 minutes before adding the DNA to cells) Put a tube of NEB DH 5 alpha E. coli cells on ice and wait until they thaw completely. Divide the cells into 50 ul aliquotes. Add 1 ul of plasmid DNA to 50 ul of cells. Mix by carefully flicking the tube. Do not vortex or pipette in and out! Place the mixture on ice for 30 minutes. Heat shock the cells at 42 °C for 30 seconds and immediately put on back on ice. Keep cells on ice for next 5 minutes. Do not mix. Pipette 950 ul of SOC media kept at room temperature into the mixture. If SOC is not available, use LB. Incubate the mixture at 37 °C for 60 minutes Prepare plates with appropriate antibiotics. Bring plates to room temperature before plating. Use 2 plates per transformation reaction. Plate 200 ul of cells on one plate. Pellet the remaining cells and resuspend in 200ul of LB. Plate the remaining cells on second plate. Incubate plates overnight at 37 °C. Place the master on the PDMS with SU-8 facing up and wait 10mins on an even surface. Pour 15:1 (wt/wt) PDMS mixture onto the silicon master and degas it in a vacuum desiccator for 30min. use 15g and 3g of PDMS for the fabrication of the upper and lower microchannel slabs. Fully cure PDMS in an oven maintained at 60c for at least 4 hours Peel the cured PDMS off the master, and cut it into a 2-cm wide x 3-cm long rectangular block using a scalpel. Punch holes through the upper channel layer by using a biopsy punch with a diameter of 2mm. Fabrication of porous membranes Pour degassed 15:1 (wt/wt) PDMS mixture into an empty petri dish to gener4ate a 1-cm-thick flat PDMS slab. Cure PDMS in a levelled dry oven at 60c for 4 h Use a scalpel to cut the fully cured PDMS along the edge of the petri dish, and remove it from the dish by using tweezers. Rinse the removed PDMS slab with 100% ethanol, and dry it completely using compressed nitrogen or air Treat the PDMS slab with oxygen plasma for 1.5 min, and salinize it in a vacuum desiccator overnight Place a silicon master containing an array of micro fabricated circular pillars (10µm in diameter and 30µm In height) with a centre to centre facing of 25µm at the centre of the bottom surface of a clean petri dish Pour 3g of degassed 15:1 (wt/wt) PDMS mixture into the wafer, spread it evenly. Avoid the formation of bubbles. Gently put a salinized PDMS slab from step 5 on the surface of the silicon master covered with uncured PDMS. Release the slab very slowly to prevent the formation of air bubbles Place a frosted glass slide on the salinized PDMS block, and place 3 kg weight on the glass slide. Wait for 30 min to allow for intimate contact between the PDMS slab and micro fabricated master surface. Perform this step on a level surface. Move to the entire assembly to a dry oven at 60c and incubate it overnight. Remove the sample from the oven, take off the weight, and cool the assembly to room temperature over 30 min Use a scalpel to lift up a corner of the slab, and slowly peel it from the wafer. Apply 100% ethanol to the gap between the PDMS surface and the silicon wafer during this step to facilitate the detachment of the PDMS layer. Alignment and Assembly of the micro device Clean the upper PDMS layer and the porous membrane with packaging tape. Do not apply excessive pressure when you are cleaning the membrane surface, in order to avoid unwanted damage of the device and delamination. Treat the membrane surface and the channel of the upper PDMS layer with the plasma by using a corona generator for 3s and 1min, respectively. Use sweeping motions to achieve uniform treatment, and keep the tip of the electrode of the corona generator 5 mm away from the sample surface for best results. Overlay the upper microchannel layer on the PDMS membrane, and bring them in contact. Press the PDMD slabs to permit intimate contact between layers and remove trapped air. Incubate the assembled layers in the dry oven at 80 co. for at least 12h Remove the sample from the oven, and cool it down at room temperature for 1h Cut along the edges of the upper PDMS channel layer bonded to the membrane by using a scalpel, and gently peel the salinized flat PDMS from the assembly. Put a few drops of 100 % ethanol between the layers for easier detachment. Tear off the portions of a porous membrane located over the lateral vacuum chambers using fine tip tweezers under a stereoscope. Expose the membrane surface and the channel side of the lower PDMS layer to the corona under a stereoscope. Align and bond the device. Attempt to pull apart the upper and lower PDMS slabs to qualitatively determine the success of device bonding.
