Dissolve 0.42 g of agarose (0.7%) or 1.2 g of agarose (2%) in 60 mL of 1 x TBE buffer. Use TBE for making analytical gels and use TAE buffer if gel is meant for DNA or plasmid extraction. Melt the agarose in the microwave until boiling. Take out the solution and mix it by shaking the bottle. Put the solution back into the microwave and let it boil again. Repeat mixing and boiling until the agarose is completely melted. Set up an agarose gel box with spacers and a comb. Take out some solution with a blue pipette tip and put it along the inside of the spacers in the gel box and let it solidify. Add 2 Î¼L of Ethidium Bromide to the remaining solution and mix by shaking the bottle. Pour the rest of the solution into the gel box. Let it stand for at least 30 minutes until the gel becomes solid. Fill the gel box up with 1x TBE (or 1x TAE) buffer so that the gel is just covered.
