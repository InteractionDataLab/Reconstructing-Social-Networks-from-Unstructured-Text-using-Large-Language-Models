To knock out abrB in Bacillus subtilis strains to obtain a more rigid biofilm. AbrB suppresses biofilm formation. Knocking out AbrB should result in a more rigid biofilm. Transformation failed, as no colonies were found on the plate. Transformation 07:07, 26 July 2015 - 00:00, 26 July 2015 B.s. 3610 comI colony was placed in MC according to protocol for Bacillus subtilis transformation. The tube was put in the shaking incubator at 37 °C. At 12:22 the OD at 600 nm was measured of the transformation culture:0.00. The sample was put back in the shaking incubator at 37 °C for overnight growth. Atze van Stralen 11:15, 27 July 2015 - 00:00, 27 July 2015 The OD at 600 nm was measured again: 0.82. 400 µL of culture was mixed with 10 µL BD1807 DNA for obtaining the abrB knock out. The sample was put in a new 15 mL tube in the shaking incubator at 37 °C. At 13:30 100 µL of culture was put on an LB plus chloramphenicol plate. This plate was put in the incubator at 37 °C. The rest of the culture was stored in the fridge at 4 °C. Atze van Stralen 11:15, 28 July 2015 - 00:00, 28 July 2015 The transformation failed, as there were no colonies on the plate.
