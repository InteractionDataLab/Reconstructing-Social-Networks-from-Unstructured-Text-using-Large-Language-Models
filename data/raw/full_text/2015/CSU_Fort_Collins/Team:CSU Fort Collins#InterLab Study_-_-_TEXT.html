BBa_J23101: 2015 Kit Plate 1, Well 20K. Size: 35 bp. BBa_I13504: 2015 Kit Plate 4, Well 21J. Size: 875 bp. With a pipette tip, punch a hole through the foil cover into the corresponding well of the part desired. Make sure you have properly oriented the plate. To prevent cross-contamination, do not remove the foil cover. Pipette 10 μl of nuclease-free water into the well. Pipette up and down a few times until mixture is homogeneous. The sample should turn red Transform BioBrick parts Set the water bath to 42 ℃ and remove 2 Cm25 plates (for part J23101) and 2 Amp100 plates (for part I13504) and allow the plates to come to room temperature. Thaw 2 tubes (1 for each part) of chemically competent E. coli cells on ice. Leave in microcentrifuge tube. For each part, add 1 ul of the resuspended DNA into the tube of competent cells. Incubate on ice for 30 minutes. Heat shock cells for 60 seconds at 42 ℃ without shaking. Place on ice for 2 minutes. Aseptically (by fire or in the hood) add 250 μl of LB media to the tube of chemically competent cells. Do not add antibiotic at this step. Cap tightly. Place tube horizontally in shaker. Incubate at 37 ℃ and 225 rpm for 1 hour. In the laminar hood, spread 100 μl of transformants onto respective LB+Antibiotic plates. Leave plates in 37 ℃ incubator overnight. Store remaining liquid cultures in 4 ℃ Confirm transformation Pick and annotate (give each colony a number or letter) 16 colonies onto new LB+Antibiotic plates – see above for respective antibiotic resistances. Leave plates in 37 ℃ incubator overnight. Verify all 16 colonies via colony PCR. Use the following mixture and thermalcycler program: Primer A: Sequencing VF2: TGCCACCTGACGTCTAAGAA [Tm = 55.9] Primer B: Sequencing VR: ATTACCGCCTTTGAGTGAGC [Tm = 55.3] Note: To add your template DNA, use a toothpick or pipette tip to touch the colony and then swirl it in the PCR mixture to add some cells into your PCR mixture. Using the ‘Ligation Template’ Excel Sheet, calculate the amount of each component to combine in a ligation mix for an Insert:Backbone ratio of 4:1 with a total DNA concentration of 7.5-10.0 ng/uL. Incubate the ligation mixture at 25 °C (room temperature – can be done on the lab bench) for 1 hour (or at 16 °C overnight). Heat inactivate at 65 °C for 10 minutes. Place on ice. Transformation Set water bath to 42 °C Remove 3 LB + Cm25 plates from 4 °C and allow them to come to room temperature. Thaw chemically competent cells on ice. Leave in microcentrifuge tube. Add 5 μl of ligation mix to chemically competent cells. Add 5 μl of digested backbone to chemically competent cells as a control. Incubate on ice for 30 minutes. Heat shock cells for 60 sec at 42 °C without shaking. Place on ice for 2 minutes. Aseptically (by the fire on in the hood) add 250 μl of LB media to the tube. Do not add antibiotic at this step. Cap tightly. Place tube horizontally in shaker. Incubate at 37 °C and 225 rpm for 1 hour. In the laminar hood, spread 100 μL of transformants onto an LB + Cm25 plate. Spin down the remaining cells (16,000 x g for 10 minutes should suffice). Remove all media and resuspend in 100 μL of LB until the mixture is homogeneous. This step isn’t necessary for the control. In the laminar hood, spread the remaining 100 μL of transformants onto an LB + Cm25 Leave plates in 37 °C incubator overnight. Store remaining liquid cultures in 4 °C.
