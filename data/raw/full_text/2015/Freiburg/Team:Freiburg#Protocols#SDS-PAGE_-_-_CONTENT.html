    SDS-PAGE

    SDS-PAGE, protein purificationremarks concerning the protocol (author, adapted from etc.)

    material: Tris, glycin, SDS, glycerin, β-mercaptoethanol, bromphenol blue, methanol, acetic acid, coomassie brilliant blue, isopropyl alcohol … duration: … min

    Buffers and solutions
    10x SDS running buffer 60 g Trisma (248 mM)
    288 g Glycin (2 M)
    20 g SDS (1% (w/v))
    ad 2 l H2O
    no need to pH, filter or degas
    dilute to 1X for running SDS-PAGE gels
    4x SDS sample buffer 40% Glycerin (w/v)
    20% β-mercaptoethanol (v/v)
    9% SDS (v/v)
    250 mM Tris (pH 6,8)
    0.5% bromphenol blue (w/v)
    Coomassie staining solution 225 ml methanol
    225 ml H20
    1.25 g Coomassie Brilliant Blue (CBB) R-250 (w/v)
    50 ml (glacial) acetic acid
    destaining solution 300 ml methanol (30% (v/v))
    100 ml acetic acid (10% (v/v))
    600 ml H20
    APS 5 g APS
    ad 50 ml H20 (10 % w/v final concentration
    make 1 ml aliquots, store at -20°C
    4x sample loading buffer 1.5 ml of 1 M Tris-HCl pH 6.8
    3 ml of 1 M DTT (dithiothreitol)
    0.6 g SDS
    0.03 g bromphenol blue
    2.4 ml glycerol
    ad 7.5 H2O
    if solution is orange/yellow in color, add 1 drops of 5 M NaOH to adjust pH
    make 500 µl aliquots and store at -20°C
    gel making buffer for resolving gel 1.5 M Tris-HCl; pH 8.8
    118 g Tris-HCl in H20
    ad 500 ml H2O
    filter and degas
    gel making buffer for stacking gel 1 M Tris-HCl; pH 6.8
    78.8 g Tris-HCl in H20
    ad 500 ml H2O
    filter and degas
