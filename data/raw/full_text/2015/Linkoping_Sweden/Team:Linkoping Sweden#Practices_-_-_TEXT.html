We were fortunate to have insightful discussions with participants in our side projects. One of them suffered from multiple food allergies, with peanuts causing the most problems, and stated the following about the public awareness of food allergies. “Lack of knowledge is the biggest problem and the ignorance stems from this lack of knowledge. How will you know of you don’t understand the consequences? How will you be able to respect it?” Becka Lexén, 22, student. It goes without saying that LiU iGEM wants to reach out to the public and raise awareness of food allergies, especially peanut allergy. We have thoroughly researched and emotionally explored the world of food allergies. Our goal is to communicate what we have learnt, and to do so in several different ways to cover as much ground as possible. Get the word out - Synthetic biology Apart from spreading information about peanut allergy, we also want to inform the public in how we by using synthetic biology can deal with real life problems. We have made a particular effort in getting in touch with the youth in our area to convey the possibilities of synthetic biology. By holding lectures for students in secondary school, high school and university we are hoping to spark their interest. Policy and Practice projects “Keep calm and go nuts, without peanuts” A market survey for people with peanut allergy. “Do you know nothing or nuthings?” A study over the common knowledge of peanut allergy among students and employees at universities. “Food Allergies - A Mini Documentary” A video where aspects of food allergy is discussed by a physician, scientists and persons dealing with food allergies. "Can I eat this?" A fun and informative children’s book about the elephant Tiny. "Science 2 Go" A concept were members of the LiU iGEM team has provided easy accessible summaries of previous research connected to our project, food allergies, biotechnology or synthetic biology.
