We all attend the Hankuk Academy of Foreign Studies. One of the most distinguishing features of our team is that we come from two different courses in our high school: International Course and Natural Science Course. The two courses offer completely different curriculum, so each of us is adapted to different ways of learning and thinking. This characteristic has sometimes caused minor conflicts within the team, and at the same time suggested us with fresh perspectives. Due to this hardships we had to face, we refer to our process as “the highway to hell”. Our process is like a highway-the more we meet and discuss ideas, the more intellectual challenge was incurred upon us. You need to fill this part out. Describe yourself in a short paragraph.” Me: “What? What. What?” Editor: “Be quick. We need to upload this. Deadline’s soon closing. At least tell me what you did!” What I did. A short moment of reflection of my life suddenly flashed an insight in my brain. Me: “I inhale oxygen.” Editor: “What? Don’t kid me. This is important!” Me: “I exhale carbon dioxide. In the process, I produce ATP to function my body.” Editor: “Not what your cell does! I am asking about what you do!” Me: “This existence, the organism I call “myself” is a mere combination of myriads of cells, performing their duty in their right places. And their only duty is synthesizing ATPs through respiration. So is mine.” The editor seemed to angrily raise a point, but as the rage in his face slowly faded, I knew that he wouldn’t be able to find an argument against this. He then fell into a state of existential questioning. Who are we, where do we come from, and what are we made of? All answers to the problems of life; all condensed into one field of science; that is, biology. Meanwhile, the editor still seems confused.
