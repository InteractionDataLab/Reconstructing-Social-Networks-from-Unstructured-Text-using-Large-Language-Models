UV microscopy verified its fluorescence. 20x magnification. 100% excitation light intensity. thoughts The fact that the fiber was stronger and thinner during this spin could be due to a number of factors: this spin was 15% w/v. Last time's spin was 12% w/v. As we wound the thread around tweezers, we were stretching the thread. This spin's fluorescence is much less than last time's spin , which was much thicker. This is despite the fact that last time's spin had a silk:tamura ratio of 4000:1, while this time's spin has a ratio of 500:1. This indicates that the thickness of fiber under the microscope does matter -- thicker threads fluoresce more due to having a greater amount of GFP per length of thread.
